2005 GP2 Season

Nico Rosberg won the championship.

GP2 replaced Formula 3000, some of the teams that competed in Formula 3000 entered GP2. The teams were Durango, Arden International, Coloni Motorsport, DAMS, Super Nova Racing and BCN Competicion. Some new teams entered the series, they were iSport International, Hitech Piquet Racing, ART Grand Prix, David Price Racing, Campos Racing and Racing Engineering.

Gianmaria Bruni and Giorgio Pantano moved to GP2 from Formula 1, Adam Carroll and Nelson Piquet moved to GP2 from British Formula 3. Heikki Kovalainen moved to GP2 from World Series by Nissan, Neel Jani and Ryan Sharp moved from Formula Renault V6 Eurocup, Scott Speed moved from Eurocup Formula Renault 2.0. Nico Rosberg moved from Formula 3 Euroseries to GP2.

2004 Formula 3000 champion Vitantonio Liuzzi had moved to Red Bull Racing in Formula 1.

2006 GP2 Season

Lewis Hamilton won the championship and ART Grand Prix were team champions.

Andrea Zuber was the first driver to race under a UAE licence.

Mike Conway moved to GP2 from British Formula 3, Luca Filippi moved from Italian Formula 3000 to GP2.

Lewis Hamilton and Lucas di Grrassi moved to GP2 from Formula Three Euroseries.

Timo Glock moved to GP2 from Champ Car World Series and Vitaly Petrov moved to GP2 from Formula 1600 Russia.

Italian team Trident entered the series.

Mathias Lauda left for DTM in Germany, Ryan Sharp left for World Touring Cars.

Heikki Kovalainen, Nico Rosberg and Scott Speed left GP2 for Formula 1, Kovalainen went to Mild Seven Renault, Rosberg to Williams and Speed went to Toro Rosso.

2007 GP2 Season

Timo Glock won the championship.

Mikhail Aleshin, Sebastien Buemi, Felipe Alburquerque, Ho Pin Tung, Kazuki Nakajima and Karun Chandhok made their debut. Ho Pin Tung was the first Chinese driver in the series and Karun Chandhok was the first Indian driver.

Piquet Sports became Minardi Piquet Sports and operated under an Italian licence.

Lewis Hamilton left GP2 for Mclaren in Fomrula 1.

2008 GP2 Season

Giorgio Pantano won the championship and Barwa International Campos Team were team champions.

Romain Grosjean, Jerome d'Ambrosio, Kamui Kobayashi, Marko Asmer and Milos Pavlovic made their debuts.

Marko Asmer wass the first Estonian driver in the series, Michael Herck was the first Romanian driver and Milos Pavlovic was the first Serbian driver.

Campos Team became Barwa International Campos Team and Minardi Piquet Sports reverted back to Piquet Sports. Arden became Trust Team Arden.

2009 GP2 Season

Nico Hulkenburg won the championship and ART Grand Prix were the team champions.

It was the first time that the series was won before the final round of the season.

Nico Hulkenburg and Edoardo Mortara moved to GP2 from Formula 3 Euro Series. Sergio Perez moved from British Formula 3 to GP2.

Giedo Van de Garde moved from Formula Renault World Series to GP2.

Mike Conway left GP2 for Indycar, Ho-Pin Tung left GP2 for A1 Grand Prix and Super League Formula. Bruno Senna left for Le Mans series.

Sebastien Buemi and Romain Grosjean left GP2 for Formula 1, Buemi went to Toro Rosso and Grosjean went to Renault to replace the sacked Nelson Piquet Jr.

Former Formula 1 driver Tiago Monteiro bought BCN Competicion team and it was renamed Ocean Racing Team.

Fisichello Motorsport became PartypokerRacing.com Scuderia Coloni, there were forced to miss round 8 at Spa due to unresolved financial disputes. Campos Grand Prix became Barwa Addax and Durango team missed round 9 and 10 due to one car having a broken chassis and not having suitably qualified drivers.

2010 GP2 Season

Pastor Maldonado won the championship and Rapox were team champions

Gp2 now had a feeder with the formation of GP3.

Ho-Pin Tung returned to the series after a season in Superleague Formula. Charles Pic, Oliver Turvey and Adrian Zaugg moved to the series from Formula Renault 3.5 series.

Formula 3 Euro Series champion Jules Bianchi and Sam Bird moved to GP2 from Formula 3 Euro Series. Max Chilton moved to Gp2 from British Formula 3, All-Japan Formula Three champion Marcus Ericsson moved to Gp2 and Brendon Hartley made his debut.

Edoardo Mortara went back to Formula 3 and Romain Grosjean returned to GP2 after replacing Nelson Piquet Jr at Renault.

Nico Hulkenburg, Vitaly Petrov, Kamui Kobayashi, Karun Chandhok and Lucas di Grassi left GP2 for Formula 1. Hulkenburg went to Williams, Petrov went to Renault, Kobayashi went to Sauber, Chandhok went to Hispania Racing and di Grassi went to Virgin Racing.

The Durango team were sidelined due to financial problems.

2011 GP2 Season

Romain Grosjean won the championship

GP3 champion Esteban Gutierrez moved to GP2 and Jolyon Palmer made his debut.

Lotus Cars lent their name to ART Grand Prix which became Lotus ART Grand Prix, Caterham Team AirAsia and Carlin entered GP2 and David Price Racing left the series. Caterham Team AirAsia was the first Malaysian team to enter GP2.

Ho-Pin Tung left GP2 for Indycar, Pastor Maldonado, Jerome d'Ambrosio and Sergio Perez left GP2 for Formula 1. Maldonado moved to Williams, d'Ambrosio to Virgin Racing and Perez went to Sauber.

2012 GP2 Season

Davide Valsecchi won the championship and DAMS were the teams champions.

British Formula 3 champion Felipe Nasr entered GP2, James Calado and Rio Haryanto moved to GP2 from GP3. Nathanael Berton moved from Formula Renault 3.5 to GP2.

Giedo Van der Garde moved from Barwa Addax team to Caterham Racing and joined the parent team Caterham F1 as a test and reserve driver.

Christian Vietoris left GP2 for DTM series in Germany. Sam Bird and Jules Bianchi left GP2 for Renault 3.5, Bianchi was also a Force India reserve driver.

Romain Grosjean and Charles Pic left GP2 for Formula 1, Grosjean went to Lotus and Pic went to Marussia.

Carlin Motorsport entered into partnership with Marussia Motors and Marussia F1. ART Grand Prix was renamed Lotus Grand Prix in GP2 and GP3 due to the relationship with their sponsors Lotus Cars. The livery changed to the black and gold used by the Lotus F1 team.

Team Lazarus replaced Super Nova who left the series and the calendar was expanded to 12 rounds including new races in Malaysia and Singapore.

2013 GP2 Season

Fabio Leimer was the championship and Russian Time were team champions.

GP3 champion Mitch Evans and Daniel Abt moved to GP2 from GP3 and Sam Bird returned to the series from Formula Renault 3.5. Qinghua Ma entered GP2 after the HRT Formula 1 team collapsed.

Luca Filippi left GP2 for Indycar in America.

Max Chilton, Esteban Gutierrez and Luiz Razia left GP2 for Formula 1, Chilton and Razia moved to Marussia, but Razia lost his seat a month later and failed to secure a GP2 seat. Gutierrez moved to Sauber.

ART Grand Prix reverted back to their original name and dropped Lotus from their name.

Three new teams entered GP2, Russian Time, Hilmer Motorsport and MP Motorsport replacing iSport International, Ocean Racing Tecchnology and Scuderia Coloni who had left the series. Russian Time purchased iSport International, Hilmer Motorsport replaced Ocean Racing Technology which withdrew from GP" and GP3 due to financial difficulties. Scuderia Coloni left the series and were replaced by MP Motorsport.

2014 GP2 Season

Jolyon Palmer won the championship and DAMS were the team champions.

Pierre Gasly and Stoffel Vandoorne moved to GP2 from Formula Renault 3.5 series and European Formula 3 champion Raffaele Marciello moved to GP2.

Formula 3 champion Nicolas Latifi replaced Daniel Abt at Hilmer Motorsport for the final round of the championship and Daniel Abt left for new racing series Formula E.

Sam Bird left GP2 for United Sportscar Championship, James Calado and Fabio Leimer left GP2 for the World Endurance Championship. Marcus Ericsson and Robin Frijns left GP2 for Formula 1, both moved to Caterham F1 team with Frijns as the test driver.

The Addax team changed back to Campos Racing as team Principal Alejandro Agag left to concentrate on orgainishing the new racing series Formula E. Hilmer Motorsport formed a partnership with Force India to become the Force India Junior Team.

2015 GP2 Season

Stoffel Vandoorne won the championship and ART Grand Prix were team champions.

GP3 champion Alex Lynn moved to GP2, Nobuharu Matsushita moved to GP2 from All-Japan Formula 3 and Sergey Sirotkin moved to GP2 from Formula Renault 3.5 series.

Nicholas Latifi left GP2 for Formula Renault 3.5 and Daniel Abt left for Formula E. Jolyon Palmer and Felipe Nasr left GP2 for Formula 1, Nasr went to Sauber and Palmer became Lotus test driver.

GP3 team Staus Grand Prix expanded and entered GP2 repllacing Caterham Racing, Virtuosi Racing took over Russian Time.

2016 GP2 Season

Pierre Gasly won the championship and Prema Racing were team champions.

It was the final GP2 season.

Luca Ghiotto moved to GP2 from GP3, Antonio Giovinazzi moved to GP2 from Formula 3.

Stoffel Vandoorne left GP2 for Super Formula in Japan and Rio Haryanto left GP2 for Manor Racing in Formula 1.

Status Grand Prix and Hilmer Motorsport left GP2, Prema Racing replaced Status Grand Prix but no replacement was found for Hilmer Motorsport.

Nobuharu Matsushita was suspended for the fourth round of the championship in Austria due to erratic driving at the Baku round.
