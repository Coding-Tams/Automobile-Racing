1960 Formula 1 Season

Jack Brabham won the drivers championship and Cooper won the constructors championship.

It was the last season a front-engined car won a Formula 1 race, for the a second season the Moroccan Grand Prix was cancelled due to monetary reasons. During the season Harry Schell, Chris Bristow and Alan Stacey died.

The Argentine Grand Prix was the last until 1972, it was the last Formula 1 race for Jose Froilan Gonzalez and Harry Schell. Ettore Chimeri was the first Venezuelan driver to race in Formula 1, the Argentine Grand Prix was the only race he took part in as he died in a crash in Havana two weeks later.

The RRC Walker Racing Team scored their first Formula 1 win at the Monaco Grand Prix, it was the first win for Lotus as a chassis constructor.

Innes Ireland and Graham Hill scored their first Formula 1 podium finish at the Dutch Grand Prix, Jim CLark made his Formula 1 debut at the race. During the Dutch Grand Prix, a spectator was killed due to being stood in a prohibited area, they were hit when Dan Gurney crashed.

At the Belgian Grand Prix the podium was all Cooper constructed cars, Olivier Gendebein was the first Belgian driver to finish on the podium. During practice Mike Taylor was injured ending his racing career, Stirling Moss was seriously injured which kept him out of the racing for a few months. On Lap 20 of the Belgian Grand Prix Chris Bristow, he lost control crashing into a 4ft embankment and was thrown from the car, he landed on barbed wire which beheaded him. Alan Stacey was hit in the face by a bird on lap 25, he crashed then somersaulted off the track and landed in a field, the car went up in flames and Alan Stacey was burnt to death.

John Surtees finished on the podium at the British Grand Prix, it was his second Grand Prix, Jim Clark finished on the Podium at the Portuguese Grand Prix

Richie Ginther won the Italian Grand Prix, it was the first victory by an American driver in Formula 1. The race was the last front-engined cars were entered. The British teams boycotted the race due to the banking used at Monza.

The Ferrari team did not enter the United States Grand Prix.

1961 Formula 1 Season

Phil Hill won the drivers championship and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

Phil Hill was the first American driver to win the championship and it was his only Formula 1 title. It was the first Formula 1 title for Ferrari. Ferrari introduced their new sharknose car which was a mid-engined car which Enzo Ferrari did not like.

Again the Moroccan Grand Prix was cancelled for monetary reasons.

Wolfgang Von Trips died during the Italian Grand Prix.

At the Monaco Grand Prix work teams and past winners were guaranteed grid places leaving nine drivers to qualify for the remaining four slots. The organisers of the Belgian Grand Prix invited 25 entries, but they were only going to pay starting money to 19 of the entries which were 16 pre selected cars plus the three fasteset of the remaining nine cars. Three of the cars without the starting money decided not to race. The top four classified cars were Ferrari's.

The French Grand Prix was very hot with the track temperatur 50 degrees and the air temperature was 39 degree. Giancarlo Baghetti won on his Formula 1 debut, it was his only Formula 1 win. Giuseppe Farina is the only other driver to win their debut race.

The British Grand Prix was the final race a rear engined car was entered, it was the last Formula 1 home race for Stirling Moss.

The German Grand Prix was the 100th Formula 1 Grand Prix, Stirling Moss won the race, it was his last Formula 1 win and the last home race for Wolfgang Von Trips. Tony Maggs made his Formula 1 debute at the German Grand Prix, he was the first South African driver, Ferrari secured the constructors championship at the race.

37 cars entered the Italian Grand Prix, a maximium of 32 could start the race. A rule was put in place enforcing a 115% cut off for qualifying, the 115% cut off time was against pole position. So to qualify drivers had to be within the 115% cut off time, Ricardo Rodriguez posted the fastest time of 2:46.4 so the cut off time was 3:11.36.

At the end of lap 2 Wolfgang Von Trips lost control and crashed into a fence into a fenced line of spectators. The race was not stopped allegedly to avoid the spectators going home en masse jamming the roads around the stadium impeding the rescue work. Phil Hill won the drivers cahmpionship at the race.

The Ferrari team again did not enter the United States Grand Prix. Roger Penske's car was painted bright Dupont Anti-Freeze yellow, the colour of his spoonsors, this was one of the first instances of commercial sponsorship in Formula 1. Innes Ireland won the race, it was his only Formula 1 win and the first win for the Lotus team.

1962 Formula 1 Season

Graham Hill won the drivers title and BRM won the constructors title.

Brabham entered Formula 1 as a constructor and Lola also enterd Fromula 1. Lotus used a monocoque frame for their cars, the first team to do so. During the season Mexican driver Ricardo Rodriguez died during a race in Mexico.

Graham Hill won the Dutch Grand Prix, it was the first Formula 1 win for the BRM team since 1959 and it was the first Formula 1 win for Graham Hill.

21 cars entered the Monaco Grand Prix, the organisers guaranteed two sports on the grid for each of the five works teams, the six remaining grid spots were left for the remaining entries to fight for. 6 of the 21 one cars did not qualify. Jim Clark scored his first Formula 1 pole position. Track marshal Ange Baldoni was killed by a dislodged right rear wheel of Richie Ginthers car, he had collided with Maurice Trintignant, Innes Ireland and trevor Taylor on lap 1.

Jim Clark won the Belgian Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first Formula 1 win for a monocoque car. Ricardo Rodriguez became the youngest driver to score points in Formula 1, the record was held for 38 years before Jenson Button broke it in 2000. Rodriguez was 20 years and 123 days, Button was 20 years and 67 days.

Ferrari did not take part in the French Grand Prix due to a metal workers strike. The race was won by Dan Gurney in a Porsche, it was the only Formula 1 win for Porsche as a constructor and the last for a German constructor until BMW Sauber in 2008. Ferrari were still sidelined for the British Grand Prix due to the metal workers strike, the race was the last held at Aintree.

Six different constructors took the first six positions on the grid at the German Grand Prix, Ferrari returned after the strike had kept them away from racing for two races. The race was delayed for over an hour due to heavy rain, the Brabham team made their Formula 1 debut.

The Italian Grand Prix was the final Formula 1 race for Ricardo Rodriguez.

Graham Hill secured his first Formula 1 championship at the South African Grand Prix and BRM won their only constructors title.

1963 Formula 1 Season

Jim CLark won the drivers championship and Lotus won the constructors championship.

New team ATS was founded by ex-Ferrari workers.

Jim CLark won most of the races during the season, his first came at the Belgian Grand Prix. New Zealander Chris Amon made his debut at the race, he was only 19 years old. Brabham scored their first Formula 1 podium. Graham Hill did not reccieve points for his third place finish at the French Grand Prix due to recieving a push start during the race. Chris Amon became the youngest driver to finish a Formula 1 race, the record stood for 38 years.

The British Grand Prix was held at Silverstone, the first time since 1960, the ATS team withdrew from the race as their cars were not ready. At the German Grand Prix the ATS team withdrew as their cars had been damaged in transit, during the race Chris Amon sustained a knee injury when the steering broke catapulting him into trees. John Surtees won the race driving for Ferrari, it was the only race during the season that was won by a non British team.

With three races to go Jim CLark clinched the constructors champoionship and Lotus clinched the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix. It was Ferrari's 100th Formula 1 race start. The drivers requested that the race be changed from the 10km bumpy circuit to the 5.75km road layout. It was the last ever Formula 1 race with a distance of more the 300 miles. Chris amon was taken to hospital after he crashed heavily during practice.

The Ferrari team appeared at the United States Grand Prix for the first time. Peter Broeker entered the race in a Stebro, he was the first Canadian to enter Formula 1 in a Canadian built car. It was the only Formula 1 race for Broeker and Stebro, they finished 7th.

1964 Formula 1 Season

John Surtees won the drivers championship and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

Peter Arundell scored his first podium finish at the Monaco Grand Prix.

Honda made their Formula 1 debut as a constructor at the German Grand Prix. Dutch driver Carel Godin de Beaufort died during practice for the race, he veered off the track and was thrown out suffering massive injuries to his head, chest and legs.

Austrian Jochen Rindt made his Formula 1 debut at the Austrian Grand Prix, he was the first Austrian driver in Formula 1.

At the Italian Grand Prix Enzo Ferrari had surrendered his entrants licence during a dispute, at the United States Grand Prix the Ferrari team was enterd by Luigi Chinetti's North American Racing Team. The cars were painted in the North American racing colours of blue and white.

The United States Grand Prix was the last race where drivers shared a car.

Going into the Mexican Grand Prix Graham Hill, John Surtees and Jim CLark were all fighting for the title. Ferrari, BRM and Lotus were all fighting for the constructors title. If Jim CLark's engine had not blown on the last laps he would have done enough to win the championship and Lotus would have won the constructors title.

1965 Formula 1 Season

Jim Clark won the drivers title and Lotus won the constructors championship.

The Austrian Grand Prix at Zeltweg Airfield was cancelled after safety complaints by teams and drivers. Jackie Stewart made his Formula 1 debut and finished third in the championship.

Jackie Stewart's debut was at the South African Grand Prix, the chequered flag was waved a lap too early at the end of the race. At the Monaco Grand Prix Team Lotus did not participate due to competing at the Indianapolis 500 in America. Jim CLark, Dan Gurney and Mike Spence missed the race. Jackie Stewart scored his first podium finish in Formula 1 at the Monaco Grand Prix.

Willy Mairesse was withdrawn from the Belgian Grand Prix for being too slow. Before the Dutch Grand Prix Lotus boss Colin Chapman punched a policeman before the race and was arrested after the race.

The drivers and constructors championships were clinched at the German Grand Prix, Jackie Stewart first Formula 1 win came at the Italian Grand Prix.

Richie Ginther won the Mexican Grand Prix, it was the only race of the season not won by a British driver or British powered car.

1966 Formula 1 Season

Jack Brabham won the drivers championship and the Brabham team won the constructors title. Jack Brabham became the first Formula 1 driver to win the championship in a car carrying his name, it was Jack Brabham's third and final championship title.

The maximum distance was reduced from 500km to 400km, the cars completing less than 90% of the race distance were not classified.

John Surtees left Ferrari and moved to Cooper and Bruce Mclaren set up his own racing team with Dan Gurney. They set up Bruce Mclaren Motor Racing and made their debut at the Monaco Grand Prix.

The new classification rules were first used at the Monaco Grand Prix, out of the 17 cars that started the race only 4 cars were classified. It was the fewest finishers in a single Formula 1 race. Jackie Stewart won the race, Lorenzo Bandini and Graham Hill finished on the podium. Guy Ligier was not classified due to only completing 75 laps by the end of the race and was 25 laps behind Jackie Stewart, Jo Bonnier was 27 laps behind Stewart. Some of the race was filmed for the movie Grand Prix.

John Surtees won the Belgian Grand Prix, it was his first win since 1964 Italian Grand Prix. Jochen Rindt scored his first podium finish. 8 cars crashed out of the race on the first lap due to a heavy rain storm, Jackie Stewart crashed into a pole and landed in a ditch. He was stuck upside down for 25 minutes until he was rescued by Graham Hill and Bob Bondurant who had gone off the track. After the race Jackie Stewart began his efforts for safer racing. Only 5 cars were classified finishers.

The French Grand Prix was the 60th Anniversary race of Grand Prix racing which had started with the French Grand Prix in 1906 and it was the last Grand Prix held on the roads near Reims. Jack Brabham won the race, it was his first win since the 1960 Portuguese Grand Prix and it was his first win driving for the Brabham team. It was the first time a Formula 1 driver won a race in a car bearing their own name.

Ferrari did not take part in the British Grand Prix due to a metalworkers strike. The Mclaren team scored their first points in Formula 1.

The German Grand Prix was open to Formula 2 cars and it was held on the Nurburgring Nordschleife. Jean-Pierre Beltoise was the higest finishing Formula 2 driver, he finished 8th overall, John Taylor died after a collision with Jacky Ickx, they crashed near the bridge. Taylor was badly burnt and died four weeks later, Jacky Ickx was a Formula 2 driver and finished 16th overall. 12 cars were classified, 4 of them were Formula 2 cars.

Jack Brabham was crowned champion at the Italian Grand Prix, the constructors title was won at the following race the United States Grand Prix.

1967 Formula 1 Season

Denny Hulme won the championship and Brabham won the constructors championship. Denny Hulme is the only New Zealander to win the World Championship.

Denny Hulme became the first of two drivers to win the championship without scoring a pole position during the season. Niki Lauda is the only other driver to repeat theis feat.

Pedro Rodriguez won the South African Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first win for a Mexican driver. The win was the last for the Cooper team. Piers Courage made his Formula 1 debut at the race which was the first Formula 1 race held at the Kyalami circuit.

On lap 82 of the Monaco Grand PRix Lorenzo Bandini clipped the chicane, hit a hidden mooring and over turned. The car exploded into flames amongst the straw bales. Bandini was trapped under the car, the rescue operation was inadequate as minutes passed before the fire was extinguished and Bandini was rescued and rushed to hospital. The downdraught from the rotor blades of the low hovering helicopter fanned the embers of the fire which reignited. Straw bales were banned from future Formula 1 races, the development of fire-retardant fuel systems and flameproof clothing for drivers and marshals accelarated. Lorenzo Bandini died three days later.

The Belgian Grand Prix was the only win for a USA built car and one of only two wins for an American-licenced constructor in Formula 1. American Dan Gurney won the race. Mike Parkes crashed on the first lap of the race, the car rolled and Parkes was thrown from the car. He was in a coma for a week and never raced in Formula 1 again.

The French Grand Prix was the only time a Formula 1 race was held at the Bugatti circuit at Le Mans. The circuit was seen as boring and unpopular with the drivers and the crowd.

Formula 2 cars took part in the German Grand Prix some of them had wooden chassis, Jackie Olivier was the highest classified Formula 2 driver, he finished fifth overall. 11 cars were classified, three were Formula 2 cars. New Zealanders Denny Hulme and Chris Amon and Australian Jack Brabham fininshed on the podium, it was the first all Australiasian podium and the first Formula 1 race since Monaco 1962 that a British driver had not fininshed on the podium.

Jack Brabham won the first Canadian Grand Prix in a tube-framed Brabham, it was the last win for the car. Dan Gurney and the Eagle team scored their last Formula 1 podium finish. Canadian Al Pease made his Formula 1 debut at the race, on the grid he changed his battery losing 6 laps, he spun later during the race and the car stalled and he suffered a flat battery. Pease ran back to the pits for a new battery and fitted the battery, at the end of the race he was 43 laps down.

The Italian Grand Prix was John Surtees last Formula 1 win. It was the first race where starting lights were used.

Denny Hulme won the championship at the Mexican Grand Prix.

1968 Formula 1 Season

Graham Hill won the drivers championship and Team Lotus won the constructors title.

Unrestricted sponsorship was allowed in Formula 1, Team Gunston painted their cars in the livery of their sponsors Gunston Cigarettes. Graham Hill's Lotus was entered in the Red, Gold and White of Imperial Tobacco's Gold Leaf brand.

Formula 2 team Tyrrell made their Formula 1 debut.

Wings were introduced and the Lotus of Graham Hill had front wings and a spoiler at the Monaco Grand Prix. Brabham and Ferrari had full width wings mounted on struts high above the driver at the Belgian Grand Prix. Lotus had a full width wing directly connected to to the rear suspensions wishbones and transmission shafts. The Matra team produced a high mounted front wing connected to the front suspension. Full face helmets were introduced, Dan Gurney was the first driver to wear one, he wore a full face helmet at the German Grand Prix and he helped invent the helmet with Bell Helmets.

Jim CLark died during a Formula 2 race at Hockenheim, he went off the track which was caused by a deflating rear tyre, 90% of the Hockenheim Circuit was made up of 2 long, slightly running through a forest. There was no protection from the trees lining the circuit on both sides, Clark crashed into a wall of trees breaking his neck killing him instantly. Mike Spence died during practice for the Indianapolis 500, Ludovico Scarfiotti died during a hillclimb event in Germany. Jo Schlesser died during the French Grand Prix.

It was the last season where all the races were run on tracks with almost no safety modifications.

Jim CLark won the South African GRand PRix, it was his final Formula 1 win and the last Formula 1 race he entered. The win was his 25th Formula 1 beating Juan Manuel Fangio's 11 year record. The race was also the final Formula 1 race for Mike Spence

The Spanish Grand Prix was the first since 1954. Mike Spence replaced Jim Clark at Lotus after CLark died during the Formula 2 race at Hockenheim, during the Indianapolis 500 practice Mike Spence was killed so Graham Hill was the only driver Team Lotus entered for the Spanish Grand Prix. Hill's car was painted red, gold and white of Imperial Tobacco's Gold Leaf brand which was the title sponsor of Team Lotus. Team Lotus was the first works team to paint their cars in sponsor livery.

Ferrari refused to attend the Monaco Grand Prix due to safety issues.

Bruce Mclaren won the Belgian Grand Prix, it was his last Formula 1 win and the first for the Mclaren team. On lap 7 of the race Brian Redman went off the circuit and crashed over the barrier into a parked car, his car caught fire and Redman managed to escape.

Matra were the first French constructor to win a Formula 1 race when Jackie Stewart won the Dutch Grand Prix.

Jochen Rindt scored his first Formula 1 pole position at the French Grand Prix, on lap 2 Jo Schlesser lost control and overturned his car caught fire partically due to its magnesium content. This prompted many safety precautions in later races. Graham Hill retired on lap 14 and gave his visor to Jo Siffert who's helmet did not have a visor.

The French Grand Prix was the last race at the Rouen-Les-Esssarts.

Jo Siffert was the first Swiss driver to win a Formula 1 Grand Prix, he won the British Grand Prix. It was also the first win for the Rob Walker Racing Team. Jack Brabham made his 106th Formula 1 start

Dan Gurney wore a full face helmet during the German Grand Prix, he was the first driver to do so. Jacky Ickx became the youngest pole sitter

Mario Andretti and Bobby Unser were meant to make their debuts at the Italian Grand Prix, they wanted to fly back to the United States for the Hoosier Hundred the following day, then fly back to Italy in time for the Italian Grand Prix. If the drivers returned to the States they would be banned from competing in the Italian Grand Prix under the ACI ruling which forbade drivers to compete in another race within 24 hours of a Grand Prix. Andretti and Unser flew back to the United States and did not return for the Italian Grand Prix.

Graham Hill, Jackie Stewart and Denny Hulme went into the Mexican Grand Prix with a chance of winning the championship. Graham Hill won the race and the championship.

1969 Formula 1 Season

Jackie Stewart won the drivers championship and Matra won the constructors title.

Matra was the only French constructors with a French chassis built in France to win the championship and the only time a constructor won without fielding a works team.

The Montjuic circuit in Barcelona was the first circuit to be Armco-lined, the Belgian Grand Prix was boycotted by many drivers due to the state of the track.

Al Pease became the only driver to be disqualified for being too slow.

Chris Amon was the only Ferrari entry at the South African Grand Prix.

Both the Lotus cars suffered massive accidents during the Spanish Grand Prix when the suspension mounted wing supports failed, the race was the last race of the high wing era. Graham Hill's win at the Monaco Grand Prix was his last Formula 1 win and his fifth Monaco win, Piers Courage scored his first Formula one podium finish. Piers Courage was driving a privately entered Brabham car enterd by Frank Williams. The Monaco Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race for Cooper as a constructor and Maserati as an engine supplier. Aerodynamic wings were banned.

From the Dutch Grand Prix wings were allowed but there were stricter regulations for them. Henri Pescarolo was the highest classified Formula 2 driver at the German Grand Prix, he fininshed fifth. 12 cars were classified at the race, 6 of them were Formula 2 cars. Gerhard Mitter was killed during the practice for the race. It was the final Formula 1 race at the Nnurburgring before it was rebuilt and fitted with Armco barriers.

Al Pease was disqualified from the Canadian Grand Prix for driving too slowly, he was the only driver disqualified from a race for driving too slow. The race was the last 1-2 finish for Brbham until 1974 United States Grand Prix.
