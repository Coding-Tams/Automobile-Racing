1985 Formula 3000 Season

Christian Danner won the championship.

Formula 3000 replaced Formula 2, it was the official feeder series for Formula 1.

Johnny Dumfries made his Formula 3000 debut, he was the 7th Marquess of Bute. He was known as Johnny Dumfries when he was Earl of Dumfries and known as John Bute when he was Marquess of Bute. Adrian Campos later became the founder of the Campos Meta Formula 1 team that later became known as HRT. He also set up a junior series team Campos Racing.

1989 International Formula 3000

Jean ALesi won the championship

Jean Alesi made his Formula 1 debut with Tyrrell in 1989. Gary Brabham, Eric Van de Poele, Eddie Irvine, JJ Lehto, Allan McNish, Erik Comas and Pedro Chaves made their Formula 3000 debut. Pedro Chaves was the first Portuguese driver in the series Pacific Racing, DAMS and Leyton House Racing entered Formula 3000.

1990 International Formula 3000

Erik Comas won the championship.

At Doningto Park Allan McNish and Emanuele Naspetti collied during the race at Donington Park, McNish went over the concrete wall, his engine broke away and killed a spectator. The final round of the season was scheduled for Dijon-Prenois but was cancelled, the official statement cited the Gulf crisis, but the French ban on tabacco sponsorship was the reason behind the cancellation.

Heinz-Harald Frentzen made his Formula 3000 debut, RSM Marko team entered Formula 3000, the first Austrian team. Paul Stewart Racing entered the series.

1991 International Formula 3000

Christian Fittipaldi won the championship.

Paul Stewart made his Formula 3000 debut, the Ralt team returned to Formula 3000, Vortex Motorsport were the first Dutch team to enter Formula 3000.

Christian Fittipaldi is the son of Wilson Fittipaldi, nephew of Emerson Fittipaldi.

1992 International Formula 3000

Luca Badoer won the championship.

Exotic fuel blend was banned. Rubens Barrichello, David Coulthard and Olivier Panis made their debuts.

Piquet Racing was the first Brazilian team to enter Formula 3000.

1993 International Formula 3000

Olivier Panis won the championship.

Pedro Diniz made his Fomrula 3000 debut, Durango, Nordic Racing and Mythos Racing entered the series.

1994 International Formula 3000

Jean-Christophe Boullion won the championship.

Four Formula 3000 races were held during Formula 1 weekends, they were the Spanish, German, Belgian and Portuguese races.

1995 Formula 3000

Vincenzo Sospiri won the championship.

Marco Campos died after suffering head injuries in a crash during the final race at Magny Cours.

1996 Formula 3000

Jorg Muller won the championship.

Every team had the same chassis, engine and tyres.

Kenny Brack was disqualified from the races at Hockenheimring for dangerous driving, Laurent Redon was disqualified from the race at Estoril due to taking the wrong position on the grid.

1997 Formula 3000

Ricardo Zonta won the championship.

Juan Pablo Montoya made his debut, Norwegian Thomas Schie made his debut, the first Norwegian driver in the series, Gonzalo Rodriguez was the first Uruguayian driver.

The Nurburgring race was stopped prematurely due to an accident involving Dino Morelli. Juan Pablo Montoya was disqualified during the Spa race for holding up the field with a damaged car.

1998 International Formula 3000

Juan Pablo Montoya won the championship.

Tomas Enge was the first Czech driver, Christian Horner and Nick Heidfeld made their debuts. Christian Horner later became the team principal of Red Bull Racing in Formula 1.

Arden Racing entered the series, West Competition team were established as the Mclaren junior team. Bob Salisbury Engineering were on the entry list only and did not compete in any races.

1998 was the last International Formula 3000 season where races were held independently from Formula 1.

1999 International Formula 3000

Nick Heidfeld won the championship.

Formula 3000 races now took place during the Formula 1 weekend as support races.

21 teams were entered, the seven bottom ranked teams would not have been eligible to advance to the next year when only 15 teams would be allowed and the teams could run a maximum of 2 cars. RSM Marko became the Red Bull Junior Team.

Viktor Maslov was the first Russian driver in the series and Alex Yoong was the first Malaysian driver.

2000 Formula 3000

Bruno Junqueira won the championship.

There was a limit of 15 teams, each team entered two cars each. One place was reseved for the winning Italian Formula 3000 team, the seven bottom ranked teams of 1999 were not eligible to advance to the 2000 season. Portman Arrows team had collapsed in 1999, RSM Marko bought Oreca's slot and became Red Bull Junior Team, Arden merged with Draco and Coloni bought the slot from Team Martello, the winners of the Italian Championship. West Competition was renamed mysap.com and became a Mclaren junior team, the second Super Nova team Den Bla Avis operated under the name Petrobas Junior Team. European Formula was confirmed as the junior team for Arrows, Team Astromega partnered with Minardi and Super Nova partnered with Benetton. Petrobas Junior Team partnered with Williams, Red Bull Junior Team partnered with Sauber and Apomatox ran under Prost as Gauloises Formula.

BAR, Jaguar, Jordan and Ferrari did not partner any Formula 3000 team.

Fernando Alonso, Sebastian Bourdais and Christijan Albers made their debut and Mark Webber was the first Australian in the series.

2001 International Formula 3000

Justin Wilson won the championship, Coca-Cola Nordic Team won the teams championship.

Justin Wilson was the first British driver to win Formula 3000.

The first Formula 3000 race to be held outside Europe was at the Autodromo Jose Carlos Pace Circuit in Brazil supporting the Brazilian Grand Prix.

Fortec Motorsport, mysap.com and World Racing Team withdrew from the series, Zsolt Baumgartner was the first Hungarian to enter the series. European Formula Racing ended its partnership with Arrows F1 team and was rebaranded European Minardi Formula 3000. Prost Grand Prix changed its name from Gauloises Formula to F3000 Prost Junior Team after sponsor Gauloises pulled out. Teams who finished 12th or higher in the championship were granted automatic entry into the 2001 season, the first three slots were allocated to new enteries or those who had won national Formula 3000 series.

Fernando Alonso left Formula 3000 for Minardi in Formmula 1, Franck Montagny had left Formula 3000 for World Series by Nissan and Christijan Albers moved to the DTM series in Germany.

2002 International Formula 3000

Sebastien Bourdais won the championship and Arden International were the team champions.

Ryan Briscoe and Tiago Monteiro made his Formula 3000 debuts.

Antonio Pizzonia was disqualified from second place at Monza for running a rear wing element upside-down. Tomas Enge won the race at the Hungaroring but was disqualified after failing a drug test.

2003 International Fomula 3000

Bjorn Wirdheim won the championship and Arden International were team champions.

Christian Montanari was the first driver from San Marino in the series, Gary Paffett and Vitantonio Liuzzi made their debuts.

Superfund ISR-Charouz, BCN F3000 and Brand Motorsport entered the series, Brand Motorsport only took part in the first race.

2004 International Formula 3000

Vitantonio Liuzzi won the championship and Arden International were the team champions.

It was the final season as Fomrula 3000.

Argentinian Jose Maria Lopez made his debut along with Mathias Lauda, Can Artam, Chanoch Nissany, Esteban Guerrieri and Alan Van de Merwe. Artam was the first Turkish driver and Nissany the first Israeli driver. Alan Van de Merwe later became the Formula 1 Medical car driver.
