1990 Formula 1 Season

Ayrton Senna won the Championship and Mclaren won the constructors title.

Alain Prost had moved to Ferrari and Nelson Piquet moved to Benetton, he would be paid $100,000 for every point scored and he also was paid a season retainer. David Brabham the son of Sir Jack Brabham made his Formula 1 debut with the Brabham Team. Martin Brundle left Formula 1 and returned to sportscar.

Arrows boss Jackie Olivier had sold the majority of the team to the Japanese Footwork Company, German teams Zakspeed and Rial had pulled out of Formula 1. Rial had folded and Zakspeed had returned to sportscar racing. Italian team Life entered Formula 1, Gary Brabham drove for them for the first two races returning to Formula 1 for the first time since 1983.

There were 19 teams and 35 cars meaning nine cars from six teams would be required to pre-qualify. The teams that had to pre-qualify were Larrousse, AGS, EuroBrun, Osella, Coloni and Life, Osella, Coloni and Life all ran just one car during the season.

At the United States Grand Prix Jean Alesi scored his first Formula 1 podium finish, Gregor Foitek started the race it was the first time he had qualified for a Formula 1 race, during 1989 he had failed to qualify 11 times.

The Interlagos circuit hosted the Brazilian Grand Prix for the first time since 1984, Gianni Morbidelli managed to qualify for the race, it was the first time in his Formula 1 career he qualified for a race.

At the 1990 Mexican Grand Prix Ayrton Senna finished in 20th poosition, the lowest classified result of his career.

Riccardo Patrese won the SAn Marino Grand Prix, it was his first win since 1983. Gary Brabham had quit the Life team and was replaced by Bruno Giacomelli who last raced in 1983. The French Grand Prix was the last held at the Paul Ricard Circuit until 2018, the Leyton House drivers Ivan Capelli and Mauricio Gugelmin had managed to qualify for the race and Ivan Capelli fininshed on the podium. It was the last podium fininsh for Ivan Capelli and the only Formula 1 podium for the Leyton House team.

Riccardo Patrese became the first driver ever to start 200 Formula 1 races when he started the British Grand Prix, Aguri Suzuki scored his first Formula 1 points at the British Grand Prix. At the German Grand Prix both the Onyx drivers managed to qualify for the race, it was the last time either car qualified for a race.

Thierry Boutsen won the Hungarian Grand Prix, it was his last Formula 1 win and the last for a driver competing under a Belgian flag. Mauricio Gugelmin managed to qualify for the Belgian Grand Prix and score points, before the race weekend the Onyx team pulled out of the championship due to financial reasons.

Ferrari announced Jean Alesi would partner Alain Prost for the 1991 season, Alesi was reported to have signed a contract with Williams but changed his mind when Ferrari showed an interest. The Spanish Grand Prix was the last to be held at Jerez, during Friday practice Martin Donnelly crashed at high speed and was thrown from the wreckage of his car and was seriously injured, this ended his Formula 1 career. The AGS cars qualified for the Spanish Grand Prix, it was the first and only time both cars qualified.It was the last Formula 1 race for Alessandro Nannini as his right arm was severed in a helicopter crash the following week.

During the Japanese Grand Prix Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost collied at the first corner ending their race, Ayrton Senna clinced the World Championship and Mclaren won their 6th championship. Aguri Suzuki scored his first Formula 1 podium fininsh at the Japanese Grand Prix, it was also his best result of his career and the only podium the Larrousse team achieved. Roberto Moreno replaced Alessandro Nannini at Benetton. Nigel Mansell announced that he was joining Williams on a two year contract from 1991. The Japanese Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race where no European driver finished on the podium. Aguri Suzuki was the last Japanese driver to finish on the podium at the Japanese Grand Prix until 2012.

JAt the Australian Grand Prix Jackie Stewart claimed Ayrton Senna was part of too many accidents for a driver of his ability.

Drivers who failed to pre-qualify in 1990 were Gabriele Tarquini, Yannick Dalmas, Claudio Langes, Gary Brabham, Bertrand Gachot, Roberto Moreno, Olivier Grouillard, Bruno Giacomelli.

Drivers who failed to qualify in 1990 were Steffan Johansson, Gianni Morbidelli, JJ Lehto, Ivan Capelli, Maurice Gugelmin, Michele Alboreto, Olivier Grouillard, Paolo Barilla, David Brabham Alex Caffi, Gabriele Tarquini, Gregor Foitek, Andrea de Cesaris, Philippe Alliot, Bertrand Gachot, Bernd Schneider.

1991 Formula 1 Season

Ayrton Senna won the championship and Mclaren won the constructors championship

It was the last season to be won by a driver from South America

Alain Prosr failed to win a race with Ferrari and he was fired before the end of the season due to a dispute. Williams re-signed Nigel Mansell and Jean Alesi moved to Ferrari, Michael Schumacher and Mika Hakkinen made their Formula 1 debut. During the season Roberto Moreno was controverdially replaced at Bennetton by Michael Schumacher. Lotus had reveterted back to its white and British racing green, they signed rookie Mika Hakkinen and Julien Bailey who had made his Formula 1 debut with Tyrrell in 1988.

The Osella team had been renamed Fondmetal, the Arrows team had been taken over by Japanese company Footwork and the team were renamed Footwork. Jordon Grand Prix was a successful Formula 3000 team and entered Formula 1, during the season Jordan driver Bertrand Gachot was jailed for two months for assaulting a London cab driver. The Modena Team entered Formula 1. Jordan, Dallara and Modena teams and entrants from Fondmetal and Coloni teams had to pre-qualify.

The United States Grand Prix was the last until 2000, it was held on a temporary road course. Mika Hakkinen made his Formula 1 debut at the race. Ayrton Senna won the Brazilian Grand Prix, it was the first time he won his home Grand Prix.

Fabrizio Barbazza made his Formula 1 debut at the San Marino Grand Prix replacing Stefan Johansson at AGS, he failed to qualify for the race. Alain Prost spun on the formation lap and did not take the start of the San Marino Grand Prix. JJ Lehto scored his first Formula 1 podium. Mika Hakkinen and Julian Bailey scored their first Formula 1 points, it was Bailey's only Formula 1 points.

Nelson Piquet's last Formula 1 win came at the Canadian Grand Prix and it was the last podium fininsh for Stefano Modena. Julian Bailey's funding ran out before the Canadian Grand Prix and he was replaced by Johnny Herbert and Stefan Johansson replaced Stefano Modena. Ferrari team manager Cesare Fiorio was fired and replaced by Piero Ferrari. Jordan team won their first Formula 1 points meaning that the team would not have to pre-qualify when the draw was reshuffled at the halfway point of the season.

The French Grand Prix moved from the Circuit Paul Ricard to Magny-Cours, Nigel Mansell won the race. It was Mansell 17th win breaking the record of Grand Prix wins by an English driver which was held by Stirling Moss. Johnny Herbert was replaced at Lotus by Michael Bartels due to Herberts Formula 3000 commitments. Satoru Nakajima announced he would retire at the end of the season.

Michael Schumacher made his Formula 1 debut at the Belgian Grand Prix, he replaced Bertrand Gachot at Jordan who was serving a two month jail sentence in Britain. Johnny Herbert had returned to Lotus for the Belgian Grand Prix. Olivier Grouillard qualified for the race and fininshed the race, Fondmetal's first finish of the season.

Michael Schumacher had moved to Benetton while still under contract to Jordan, Michael Bartels was back at Lotus filling in for Johnny Herbert at the Italian Grand Prix. Michael Schumacher scored his first Formula 1 points at the Italian Grand Prix, it was the last time Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Nelson Piquet and Nigel Mansell all finished a race in the top six.

The Spanish Grand Prix was held at the Circuit de Catalunya for the first time, Roberto Moreno was replaced at Jordan by Alessandro Zanardi and Fondmetal had sacked Olivier Grouillard who was replaced by Gabriele Tarquini. Grouillard moved to AGS and took the seat of Tarquini had vacated.

It was the fifth year in a row that the title was decided at the Japanese Grand Prix, Ayrton Senna and Nigel Mansell were both battling for the title, Senna needed to beat Mansell. Ayrton Senna finished second in the race behind winner Gerhard Berger. Ivan Capelli had been replaced at Leyton Hous by Karl Wendlinger, Ivan Capelli became spare driver at Ferrari. AGS had ran out of money and did not enter the race, Coloni had hired local driver Naoki Hattori, Martin Brundle scored Brabham's final Formula 1 points and Stefano Modeno scored Tyrrells last Formula 1 points. It was the last time Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Nelson piquet and Nigel Mansell raced together. Senna won his third World Championship.

The Australian Grand Prix was stopped on lap 16 of 81 laps, confusion followed causing the official results to be declared from the end of lap 14, it was the shortest Formula 1 race ever. Mclaren clinched the constructors championship, Nigel Mansell was ubable to take part in the podium ceremony following a crash at the end of the race. It was the last Formula 1 race for Nelson Piquet, Satoru Nakajima, Naoki Hattori, ALex Caffi and Emanuele Pirro. Alain Prost's Ferrari contract had been terminated before the Australian Grand Prix and he was replaced by Gianni Morbidelli, Roberto Moreno took Morbidelli's seat at Minardi. Bertrand Gachot returned to Formula 1 after his jail sentence and replaced Eric Bernard at Larrousse. The Coloni team entered their final Formula 1 race, new owner Andrea Sassetti confirmed that the team would be reborn as Andrea Moda Formula.

The drivers who failed to pre-qualify in 1991 were Eric Van de Poele, Nicola Larini, Pedro Chaves, Olivier Grouillard, Emanuele Pirro, Michele Alboreto, Gabriele Tarquini, Fabrizio Barbazza, Mark Blundell, Naoki Hattori.

The drivers who failed to qualify in 1991 were Alex Caffi, Stefan Johansson, Michele Alboreto, Julian Bailey, Fabrizio Barbuzza, Gabriele Tarquini, Mark Blundell, Johnny Herbert Eric Comas, Mika Hakkinen, Eric Van de Poele, Michael Bartels, Aguri Suzuki, Nicola Larini

1992 Formula 1 Season

Nigel Mansell won the Championship and Williams won the constructors title.

Nigel Mansell was the first driver to win nine races in a season, he was the first British driver to win the championship since James Hunt in 1976. The championship was won with five races to go.

Michael Schumacher finished third in the championship in his first full season, Martin Brundle partnered Schumacher at Benetton. Female driver Giovanna Amati was signed by the Brabham team. The Coloni team had been renamed Andrea Moda Formula.

The teams that were required to pre-qualify were the Larrousse team, a Fondmetal and a Footwork car and the Andrea Moda team. At the South African Grand Prix the Moda teamwere excluded so pre-qualifying was cancelled. Andrea Moda fashion company was owned by Andrea Sassetti who bought the Coloni team, it was ruled that Andrea Moda was a new team and not a continuation of the Coloni team, so the team had to pay $100,000 guarantee. Sassetti believed he did not have to pay the guarantee for the new teams as he believed to have purchased an existing team. Officials decided he had not purchased Coloni's entry into Formula 1 or the constructor itself, only its cars and facilities. Andrea Sassetti lodged an appeal citing the fact several other teams such as March, Fondmetal and Footwork had not been required to pay the guarantee when the teams changed hands.

Michael Schumacher scored his first Formula 1 podium at the Mexican Grand Prix, again pre-qualifying was cancelled due to the andrea Moda team cars not being ready in time. It was the last Mexican Grand Prix until 2015.

Andrea Moda had fired drivers Alex Coffi and Enrico Bertaggio and signed Roberto Moreno and Perry McCarthy. McCarthy had hastily acquired a super licence but when he arrived at the circuit for the Brazilian Grand Prix the race director, Roland Bruynseraede told him there had been an error in the issuing of the licence, so McCarthy was withdrawn from the race. Ivan Capelli scored his first points as a Ferrari driver.

At the Spanish Grand Prix Damon Hill made his Formula 1 debut with the Brabham team, he replaced Giovanna Amati who Brabham had dismissed. Perry McCarthy had managed to regain his super licence, but he failed to pre-qualify along with his team mate Roberto Moreno.

At the San Marino Grand Prix Nigel Mansell became the first Formula 1 driver to win the first five races of the season. At the Monaco Grand Prix Roberto Moreno managed to qualify for the race, the only time the Andrea Moda team qualified for a race.

The Andrea Moda team experienced more problems at the Canadian Grand Prix when the engines for the cars had been withheld by the freight forwarding agentfor non-payment of debts. The team had to borrow an engine from the Brabham team, it allowed Roberto Moreno to take part in pre-qualifying. The Ligier team scored their first Formula 1 points since 1989.

The Andrea Moda team failed to arrive at the French Grand Prix as the teams transporter was stuck in traffic to to a blockade by French lorry drivers. All the other teams were affected by the blockade but only Andrea Moda failed to arrive meaning that pre-qualifying was not needed. Martin Brundle scored his first Formula 1 podium finish, it was the only the second time since 1981 that Alain Prost had not appeared on the podium at the French Grand Prix.

The German Grand Prix was the last time Andrea Chiesa entered a Formula 1 race, he failed to qualify for the race. Perry McCarthy was excluded from the race due to missing a weightcheck.

Nigel Mansell clinched the World Championship title at the Hungarian Grand Prix.

Eric Van de Poele replaced Andrea Chiesa at Fondmetal, he managed to qualify for the race. Brabham reduced their entry to one car so Larrouse driver Bertrand Gachot no longer required to pre-qualify. It was the last time in Formula 1 that pre-qualifying was held. Andrea Moda did not make a serious attempt to run their second car, Roberto Moreno used both cars to pre-qualify and Perry McCarthy was only allowed to leave the pits 45 seconds before the end of the session leaving no chance to reach the start-finish line to begin a timed lap before the chequered flag was waved. Andrea Moda Formula was warned to make proper efforts to run both cars at the Belgian Grand Prix or face suspension from the Italian Grand Prix. After the Hungarian Grand Prix the Brabham team withdrew from Formula 1.

Damon Hill's first Formula 1 start came at the British Grand Prix, Michele Alboreto was replaced in the pre-qualifying pool by Gabriele Tarquini meaning both the Fondmetal drivers now were required to pre-qualify.

Ferrari's 500th Formula 1 start came at the Belgian Grand Prix.

Michael Schumacher won the Belgian Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first win for a German driver since Jochen Mass in 1975. Emanuele Naspetti made his Grand Prix debut replacing Paul Belmondo at March. It was the last race for Andrea Moda Formula team, Perry McCarthy crashed during qualifying and reported to the team he suspected that the steering rack was flexing and the steering was jammed. Team boss Andrea Sassetti replied that the team knew the rack was faulty, McCarthy quit the team. Andrea Sassetti was arrested in the paddock on the Saturday afternoon of the Belgian Grand Prix on allegations of forging documments and fraud. Erik Comas had a heavy crash during practice and was withdrawn from the race.

The Andrea Moda Formula team had been excluded from Formula 1 for 'conduct prejucial to the sport', the team arrived at the Italian Grand Prix but were refused entry to the Monza circuit. Nigel Mansell announced his retirement from Formula 1, a few minutes before the press conference he was told he could partner Prost the next season, but Mansell still announced his retirement. Rumours were circulating that Williams had signed Alain Prost for the following season and were attempting to sign Ayrton Senna.

Nigel Mansell won the Portuguese Grand Prix setting a new record for the most wins and points in a season with nine wins and 108 points. It was the first time since 1987 that there was no pre-qualifying and all the cars started the race. The Fondmetal team did not arrive at the Estoril circuit due to financial problems, the third race in succession a team exited Formula 1.

Nicola Larini replaced Ivan Capelli at Ferrari, Karl Wendlinger left March to join Sauber, Jan Lammers made his first Formula 1 start for over 10 years. Christian Fittipaldi scored his first Formula 1 points at the Japanese Grand Prix and it was the only points of the season for Minardi.

Jordan scored their only point of the season at the Australian Grand Prix, Thierry Boutsen scored his last points of his Formula 1 career. Benetton became the first team to score points in every round of the season since Lotus in 1963. It was the last race for Jan Lammers, Stefano Modena, Mauricio Gugelmin and Olivier Grouillard, also the last Formula 1 race for the March team.

Drivers who failed to qualify during 1992 were Paul Belmondo, Andrea Chiesa, Stefano Modena, Giovanna Amati, Aguri Suzuki, Eric Van de Poele, Ukyo Katayama, Damon Hill Mika Hakkinen, Christian Fittipaldi, Alessandro Zanardi, JJ Lehto

Drivers who failed to pre-qualify during 1992 were Perry McCarthy, Roberto Moreno, Ukyo Katayama

1993 Formula 1 Season

Alain Prost won the drivers coanpionship and Williams won the constructors championship.

Alain Prost won his fourth drivers title and Williams won their sixth constructors title.

The European Grand Prix returned to the calendar, it was held to replace the Pacific Grand Prix that was meant to be held at the Autopolis Circuit in Japan. The race was held at Donnington Park in the UK, the Mexican Grand Prix was dropped from the calendar and did not return until 2015.

The March team were on the entry list but did not contest any races, Alain Prost returned to Formula 1 after a year out, it was his last season as he retired at the end of the season. Damon Hill moved to Williams to partner Alain Prost, Nigel Mansell had left Formula 1 for Indycar in America. Mclaren signed Michael Andretti and Mika Hakkinen, they also signed Ayrton Senna on a race by race basis partnering Andretti. Mika Hakkinen replaced Andretti after the Italian Grand Prix. Riccardo Patrese partnered Michael Schumacher at Benetton and Gerhard Berger joined Jean Alesi at Ferrari. Martin Brundle and Mark Blundell were signed by Ligier, the only time that the team did not have any French drivers. Derek Warwick returned to Formula 1 after two years in Sports car, he was signed by the Footwork Arrows team. Luca Badoer made his Formula 1 debut driving for Scuderia Italia, Philippe Alliot returned to Formula 1 and was signed to partner Erik Comas at Larrousse. Sauber entered Formula 1 and signed JJ Lehto and Karl Wendlinger. Brabham, Andrea Moda and Fondmetal had all failed to complete the 1992 season and had folded, the March team formally withdrew at the beginning of the season. This left 13 teams and 26 drivers on the grid.

The South African Grand Prix was the first time since 1971 that the first race of the season did not feature the defending drivers champion. JJ Lehto scored Saubers first Formula 1 points. The race was the last South African Grand Prix and the last held in Africa.

Aryton Senna won the Brazilian Grand Prix, the last Brazilian driver to win on home soil until Felipe Massa in 2006. The first European Grand Prix was held since 1985 and the only Formula 1 race held at Donnington Park. Thierry Boutsen replaced Ivan Capelli at Jordan. Sega sponsored the European Grand Prix, on the podium Ayrto Senna was given a trophy that depicted Sonic the Hedgehog, he was then given the official trophy. Alain Prost, Ayrton Senna and Michael Schumacher were all on the podium at the Spanish Grand Prix, the only time they were all on the podium together.

Ayrton Senna broke Graham Hills Monaco record by winning his sixth Monaco Grand Prix. He equalled the all-time record for wins at a single Formula 1 Grand Prix.

James Hunt commentated on the Canadian Grand Prix, it was his last commentary as he died of a heart attack 48 hours after the race.

At the French Grand Prix Alain Prost scored his 100th Formula 1 podium finish, he was the first driver to achieve the milestone. Former Formula 1 driver Jonathan Palmer became a commentator for the BBC alaongside Murray Walker.

Damon Hill's first Formula 1 win came at the Hungarian Grand Prix, he was the first son of a World Champion to win a Formula 1 race. Riccardo Patrese scored his final podium finish at the race, it was the last race for Thierry Boutsen.

Thierry Boutsen retired from Formula 1 after the Belgian GRand Prix, Alessandro Zanardi failed to qualify for the Belgian Grand Prix due to a huge crash during Friday practice.

Prior to the Italian Grand Prix Michael Andretti and Mclaren had agreed to part ways, the race was his last Formula 1 Grand Prix. Damon Hill first three wins came at consecutive Grand Prix, a fest only repeated by Mika Hakkinen. Jordan signed Marco Apicella to replace Thierry Boutsen, it was Apicella's only Formula 1 start and his race only lasted 800m. Portuguese driver made his Formula 1 debut at the Italian Grand Prix, at the chequered flag Christian Fittipaldi left front wheel made contact with Pierluigi Martini rear wheel, the contact launched Fittipaldi's car into the air and the car completed a back flip, landed on its wheels and skidded across the line, both drivers were unhurt.

Alain Prost clinched the championship at the Portuguese Grand Prix and Williams clinched the constructors title. The BMS Scuderia Italia team withdrew from the championship after the race, Mika Hakkinen repllaced Michael Andretti at Mclaren and out qualified team mate Ayrton Senna. Mika Hakkinen's first podium finish came at the Japanese Grand Prix, Eddie Irvine made his Formula 1 debut at the race, after Ayrton Senna punched Eddie Irvine for unlapping himself during the race. Senna earnt a suspended two race ban.

The Australian Grand Prix was the last race for Alain Prost, Riccardo Patrese and Derek Warwick, Patrese had competed in 256 Formula 1 races. Ayrton Senna won the race, his last Formula 1 win and the last win for a Brazilian driver until 2000. It was the last race without refuelling during races until 2010 and the last victory and pole for Mclaren until 1997. Mclaren had become the most successful Formula 1 constructor in the number of wins until 1995.

1994 Formula 1 Season

Michael Schumacher won the championship and Williams won the constructors championship.

Williams won their seventh championship and Michael Schumacher won his first World Championship.

46 drivers competed during the season, 14 made their debuts and Nigel Mansell returned to Formula 1. A number of the drivers were pay drivers. Mercedes returned to Formula 1 for the first time since 1955, they returned as an engine supplier. Ferrari had their first win since 1990 and Mclaren had their first winless season since 1980. It was the final season for the original Team Lotus which was one of the most successful constructors in Formula 1.

David Coulthard, Jos Verstappen, Mika Salo, Olivier Panis, Heinz-Harald Frentzen made their Forumula 1 debuts. Ayrton Senna replaced Alain Prost at Williams, it was later proved in an interview that Senna's move to Williams was the factor behind Prosts retirement from Formula 1. It was the second season in succession that the reigning World Champion would not defend the title. Martin Brundle replaced Senna at McLaren and JJ Lehto replaced the retired Riccardo Patrese at Benetton. Formula 1 rookie Heniz-Harald Frentzen replaced JJ Lehto at Sauber.

It was the last season for the Larrousse team, Footwork and Lotus were facing financial problems, Simtek and Pacific made their Formula 1 debut and BMS merged with Minardi changing to BMS Minardi. Camel withdrew from Formula 1 and Rothmans became Williams new sponsors and cigarette brand Mild Seven sponsored the Benetton team.

Austrian driver Roland Ratzenberger and Ayrton Senna died during the San Marino Grand Prix and there were injuries to drivers, mechanics, track marshal and spectators. The FIA made sweeping changes to the rules and regulations to improve safety.

Heinz-Harald Frentzen, Olivier Panis, Jos Verstappen, Olivier Beretta and Roland Ratzenberger made their Formula 1 debuts at the Brazilian Grand Prix. Simtek and Pacific Racing teams entered Formula 1. On lap 35 their was a four car pile up, Eddie Irvine and Jos Verstappen were coming up to lap Eric Bernard. All three were rapidly closing in on Martin Brundle who had suffered engine failure. Irvine moved left as Verstappen attempted to overtake on the left forcing Verstappen onto the grass, he lost control and somersaulted over Brundle, Bernard took avoiiding action to the right. Brundle's helmet was impacted by Jos Verstappen, Brundle escaped serious injury, Eddie Irvine was sanctioned by the FIA with a one-race ban which was increased to three after an appeal by jordan failed due to Irvine already under investigation to an incident with Senna the year before.

Nicola Larini replaced the injured Jean Alesi at Ferrari for the Pacific Grand Prix, during the weekend Larini leaked to the Italian media he had used traction control during practice. Later Ferrari and Larini denied the claims. The Jordan team's first podium finish came at the Pacific Grand Prix.

The San Marino Grand Prix was a very dark weekend for formula 1. Nicola Larini scored his first Formula 1 points, Andrea de Cesaris replaced Eddie Irvine who was serving a three race suspension. JJ Lehto took his seat at Benetton, Jos Verstappen had replaced Lehto for the first two races of the season as Lehto was injured. During qualifying on the Friday Rubens Barrichello hit a kerb at 225 km/h launching him into the air, he hit the top of the tyre barrier which knocked him unconscious, the car rolled several times coming to rest upside down. He was taken to hospital for routine tests, he suffered a sprained wrist and a broken nose and had to sit out the rest of the weekend. On the Saturday the final qualifying session Roland Ratzenberger crashed hitting a concrete barrier wall almost head on and critically injured by the force of the impactwhich inflicted basal skull fracture. The session was stopped and Ratzenberger was airlifted to the same hospital Barichello had been treated at. The session was restarted 50 minutes later and serveral teams took no further part in the session. Later it was announced that Rolan Ratzenberger had died.
JJ Lehto stalled on the way to the grid, Pedro Lamy hit Lehto causing bodywork and tryes to fly into the air, parts went over the safety fencing causing injuries to a police officer and 8 spectators. The safety car was deployed, during the safety car period Eric Comas and Eric Bernard made contact, Comas pitted for repairs, once the track was clear the safety car withdrew and racing restarted. On lap 7 at 305 km/h Ayrton Senna could not make the turn at the corner and continued straight into the unprotective concrete barrier at 211km/h. Senna was killed instantly and the race was red flagged at 14:17 local time and the cars returned to the pits. 10 minutes after the race was red flagged, the Larrousse team sent Eric Comas to the end of the pit lane despite the circuit being closed. After confusion the pit lane marshal allowed Comas onto the circuit, marshal frantically waved him down as he approached the scene of the accident. Comas avoided hittin any people or cars, after seeing the accident scene Eric comas withdres from the race.
The race was restarted at 14:55 local time after Ayrton Senna was flown to hospital.

After the San Marino Grand Prix the race regulations changes were made to slow Formula 1 cars down before they collide with a wall and added large run off areas. New safety measures were put in force, the entry and exit of the pitlane were controlled by a curve to force the cars to run at a reduced speed and no team mechanic was allowed onto the pitlane except for pit stops. The Grand Prix Drivers Association was reformed, it had been disbanded in 1982 after being founded in 1961.

Legal proceedings were brought against Frank Williams, Patrick Head and Adrian Newy of Williams and Fedrico Bendinelli, a representative of Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari, Giorgio Poggi, the circuit director and Roland Bruynseraede who was the race director and sanctioned the circuit. All six were cleared of manslaughter. On 22 November 1999 an appeal absolved Head and Newy of all charges, the last appeal was heard on 13 April 2007, it was determined that the accident was caused by a steering column failure. Patrick Head was found responsible for the accident, he was not arrested as the verdict was past the statute of limitation for manslaughter.

At the Monaco Grand Prix Grand Prix an 80km/h pit lane speed limit was brought into force.

The Sauber team withdrew from the race and the Williams and Simtek teams entered only one car each. It was the last Formula 1 race for Nigel Mansell, Christian Fittipaldi, Michele Alboreto, David Brabham, JJ Lehto and Paul Belmondo. It was the last Formula 1 race where the number of entrants exceeded the number of places on the starting grid.

Michael Schumacher became Germany's first Formula 1 champion and Williams won the constructors title.

Drivers who failed to qualify during 1994 season were Roland Ratzenberger, Paul Belmondo and Bertrand Gachot.

1996 Formula 1 Season

Damon Hill won the World Championship and Williams won the constructors title.

It was the 50th Formula 1 racing season.

Damon Hill became the first son of a former Formula 1 champion to win the championship.

Benetton oficially became an Italian constructor, Benson and Hedges became Jordon's title sponsor, Tyrrell lost their title sponsor Nokia and became known as Tyrrell Yamaha. Forti lost their sponsorship of the Italian dairy corporation Parmalat. Scuderia Italia ended their working relationship with Minardi, Larrousse and Pacific left Formula 1.

Michael Schumacher and Eddie Irvine moved to Ferrari, Jean Alesi and Gerhard Berger moved to Benetton and Johnny Herbert joined Sauber. Rookie Jacques Villenuve joined Damon Hill at Williams, David Coulthard moved to Mclaren, Martin Brundle left Ligier for Jordan and Pedro Diniz moved to Ligier to partner Olivier Panis. Giancarlo Fisichella missed several races due to his International Touring Car Championship commitments, he was replaced by European Formula 3000 driver Tarso Marques. After the British Grand Prix the Forti team were declared bankrupt. The starting lights system was replaced with a new system of red lights turning on sequentaually then going out simutaneously. The 107% rule was introduced meaning that cars had to be within 107% of pole position time to qualify. Needle-like nosecone with a sharp point were banned in favour of blunter one and wraparound head restraints were introduced to prevent head injuries.

The Australian Grand Prix had moved to Melbourne, it was the second Grand Prix in a row to be held in Australia, the last race of the 1995 season was the Australian Grand Prix. Tarso Marques and Pedro Diniz had their qualifying times disallowed, Marques for a push-start and Diniz for missing the weight check and both had to start from the back of the grid at the Brazilian Grand Prix.

Andrea Montermini failed to qualify for the San Marino Grand Prix due to his time exceeding 107% time of 1:32.972.

Williams seccured the constructors title at the Hungarian Grand Prix

The race was the last for Martin Brundle, Pedro Lamy and Giovanni Lavaggi and the last for the Ligier team.

Tyrrell withdrew Ricardo Rosset from qualifying at the German Grand Prix after he crashed during free practice. Athe the Hungarian Grand Prix Rosset again failed to qualify.

Damon Hill won the Belgian Grand Prix, it was his last Formula 1 podium and the first win for the Jordan team.

Both Ralf and Michael Schumacher finished on the podium at the Italian Grand Prix, it was the first time brothers had stood on the podium together, Michael won the race and Ralf finished third. The race was the 600th start for the Ferrari team in the World Championship.

Mika Hakkinen was confirmed Formula 1 World Champion at the Japanese Grand Prix and Mclaren won the constructors title. It was the last Formula 1 race for the Tyrrell team and Riccardo Rosset.

1999 Formula 1 Season

Mika Hakkinen won the championship and Ferrari won the constructors title. It was the first constructors title for Ferrari since 1983.

The wheels were required to be tethered to the chassis.

British American Tobacco bought the Tyrell team and the name changed to British American Racing (BAR). Jacques Villenuve moved to BAR and was partnered with GTI champion Ricardo Zonta. Alessandro Zanardi returned to Formula 1 and Heinz-Harald Frentzen moved to Jordan, Ralf Schumacher moved to Williams. Pedro de La Rosa made his Formula 1 debut with the Arrows team, Pedro Diniz moved to Sauber, Johnny Herbert moved to Stewart. Luca Badoer returned to Formula 1 and signed with Minardi his team mate was rookie driver Marc Green, the Open Fortuna by Nissan champion.

Eddie Irvine won the Austalian Grand Prix, his first Formula 1 win, Marc Gene failed to set a time within 107% of pole but was allowed to start the race.

Stephane Sarrazin made is Formula 1 debut, it was his only Formula 1 race, he replaced injured Luca Badoer for the Brazilian Grand Prix. Luca Badoer returned at the San Marino Grand Prix it was his 35th Formula 1 start without scoring any points. Michael Schumacher won the San Marino Grand Prix, it was Ferrari's first win at Imola since 1983.

Winning the Monaco Grand Prix Michael Schumacher broke Nikki Lauda's record of wins for Ferrari win 16 wins. Again the wrong flag was flown for Eddie Irvine, the Irish flag was flown instead of the Union Jack.

During the Canadian Grand Prix there were four crashes in turn 13, the safety car was deployed everytime and the race finished behind the saefty car. Jarno Trulli spun and collected Jean Alesi and Rubens Barrichello, this deployed the safety car, after Ricardo Zonta spun into the wall the safety car was deployed again. It was not deployed when Damon Hill crashed as he managed to pull off the track in a safe location, on lap 30 Michael Schumacher hit the wall at turn 13 and Jacque Villenuve hit the was on lap 35.

Five cars failed to qualify for the French Grand Prix due to poor weather but the stewards chose to waive the rule due to the spacing of the field caused by weather problems. Damon Hill announced that he would retire at the end of the season.

Mclaren won the British Grand Prix for the first time since 1989, during the race Michael Schumacher crashed due to a brake failure and broke both his legs

Eddie Irvine won the Austrian Grand Prix, it was his second Formula 1 win and Ferrari's first Austrian win since 1970. Mika Salo replaced the injured Michael Schumacher at Ferrari.

Marc Gene scored Minardi's first Formula 1 points since 1995 Australian Grand Prix at the European Grand Prix, Johnny Herbert won the race, it was his final Formula 1 win and the only Formula 1 win for Stewart Grand Prix and their only double podium. It was the last time until 2003 Brazilian Grand Prix that both Ferrari drivers failed to score points.

A new Grand Prix was held, the Malaysian Grand Prix at the Sepang International Circuit, Michael Schumacher returned from injury in time for the race. Eddie Irvine won the race, it was the last win for a British driver in a Ferrari in Formula 1.

Mika Hakkinen won the drivers championship at the Japanese Grand Prix, Ferrari were confirmed as the constructors champions, their first since 1983. It was the last Formula 1 race for Stewart Grand Prix, Alessandro Zanardi and Damon Hill.
