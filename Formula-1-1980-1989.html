1980 Formula 1 Season

Alan Jones won the drivers championship and Williams won the constructors championship.

Williams won their first constructors title, Alan Jones was the first Australian to win the championship since Jack Brabham in 1966. Alain Prost and Nigel Mansell made their Formula 1 debuts. During the season their was ongoing dispute between the FISA and the FOCA concerning the ground effect aerodynamics.

Keke Rosberg and Nelson Piquet scored their first Formula 1 podium finishes at the Argentine Grand Prix. The drivers threatened to boycott the race because of the appalling state of the track which was breaking up in the infield. During the race many drivers spun off.

The Brazilian Grand Prix was origianlly meant to be held at Jacarepagua but parts of the circuit were sinking into the soft swamp land. There was a last minute switch to Interlagos which had to be resurfaced and there was heavy rebuild with new pit facilities and safety measures. A number of the drivers wanted to boycott the event due to safety concerns as the catch-fencing and barriers were not adequate enough. The race went ahead. The race was the last held at Interlagos until 1990, Rene Arnoux won the race, it was his first Formula 1 win.

The South African Grand Prix was an all French podium including drivers and teams, it was the first time since the 1968 United States Grand Prix that all the drivers on the podium were from the same country. It was the last Formula 1 race for the Shadow team.

Nelson Piquet first Formula 1 win came at the United States Grand Prix West at Long Beach, his son Nelson Piquet Jr won the Formula E race at Long Beach in 2015.

Didier Pironi won the Belgian Grand Prix, he was the fourth different winner in five races. Carlos Reutmann won the following Grand Prix at the Monaco Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win since 1978 and he was the fifth different driver to win in six races.

The Shadow team entered the French Grand Prix but failed to qualify, it was the final Formula 1 entry for the team. The team had been sold two months earlier to Macau based business man Teddy Yip.

The Ram team entered the British Grand Prix with old Williams car, Brabham had replaced Ricardo Zunino with Hector Rebaque. Jacques Laffites won the German Grand Prix, it was his fourth Formula 1 win and his first since 1979 Brazilian Grand Prix. Patrick Depailler died 10 days before the race during a private test event at Hockenheimring.

Jean-Pierre Jabouille final Formula 1 win was at the Austrian Grand Prix, it was his first win since the 1979 French Grand Prix, Jochen Mass crashed and rolled his car during the practice and was injured.

Vittorio Brambilla took Patrick Depailler's place at Alfa Romeo from the Dutch Grand Prix. Manfred Winkelhock made his Formula 1 debut replacing the injured Jochen Mass at Arrows for the Italian Grand Prix, but failed to qualify for the race. The Italian Grand Prix was held at Imola for the first time, it was the first time since 1948 that the race had not been held at Monza.

Williams secured the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix and Alan Jones won the drivers title at the Canadian. Alfa Romeo replaced Vittorio Brambilla who retired from Formula 1 after the Italian Grand Prix, with Andrea de Cesaris for the Canadian Grand Prix. Andrea de Cesaris and Mike Thackwell made their Formula 1 debut at the race. Mike Thackwell became the youngest driver to start a Formula 1 race, breaking the previous record held by Ricardo Rodriguez, Thackwell was 19 years old, his record stood for 29 years when it was broken in 2009 by Jaime Alguersuari. Nelson Piquet and Alan Jones collied at the first turn causing a multi-car pile up which involved Jean-Pierre Jarier, Derek Daly, Emerson Fittipaldi, Keke Rosberg, Mario Andretti, Gilles Villenuve and Jochen Mass. Piquet, Fittipaldi, Villenuve, Andretti and Mass all restarted the race in the spare cars. Jean-Pierre Jabouille crashed haevily during the race and seriously injured his legs and had to be cut from the car.

The United States Grand Prix was the last to be held for 9 years and the last Formula 1 race at Watkins Glen. Alain Prost missed the race due to crashing heavily in practice. It was the last Formula 1 race for Emerson Fittipaldi and Jody Scheckter.

Drivers who failed to qualify during the season were David Kennedy, Stefan Johansson, Jan Lammers, Eddie Cheever, Stephen South, Geoff Lees, Keke Rosberg, John Watson, Riccardo Zunina, Tiff Needell, Desire Wilson, Rupert Keegan, Harald Ertl, Mike Thackwell, Nigel Mansell, Manfred Winkelhock, Jody Scheckter, Kevin Cogen and Mike Thackwell.

1981 Formula 1 Season

Nelson Piquet won the drivers title and Williams won the constructors title.

Nelson Piquet won his first World Championship.

The inagural Formula 1 World Championship replaced the World Championship of Drivers and the International cup for constructors. The teams were now required to lodge entries for the entire championship season, the FIA also set the prize money for all the races. Alain Prost had moved from Mclaren to Renault, John Watson took Prost's place at Mclaren. Ligier had changed name to Talbot, Jean-Pierre Jarier took Jean-Pierre Jabouille's seat at Talbot. Derek Daly moved to March from Tyrrell, Mario Andretti left Lotus for Alfa Romeo, Nigel Mansell replaced him at Lotus. The Tyrrell team were without sponsorship and the Arrows team had new sponsorship from Ceramiche Ragno and Beta. Due to financial difficulties the United States Grand Prix was dropped, the South African Grand Prix was stripped of its championship status. The race went ahead under Formula Libre rules due to ongoing FISA-FOCA wars. The FISA were the Federation Internationale du Sport Automobile, the FOCA were the Formula 1 Constructors Association.

Most of the teams lodged a protest over the Lotus twin chassis that they debuted at the United States Grand Prix, the cars were allowed to take part in Friday practice but were banned from the rest of the weekend. It was Riccardo Patrese's first Formula 1 pole and the only pole for the Arrows team. The Tyrrell team failed to qualify, it was the first time the team failed to qualify for a race. American drivers Mario Andretti and Eddied Cheever finished in the points, it was the first time since 1975 that two American drivers had finished in the points. Chico Serra was the only rookie to qualify for the United States Grand Prix.

Formula 1 had moved to the Jacarepagua Circuit from the Interlagos Circuit for the Brazilian Grand Prix. Colombian Ricardo Londono was denied a superlicense so was not allowed to participate in the raceweekend. Argentinian Carlos Reutemann won the race by ignoring pit signals to give up the lead to his team mate and the number one driver for the team, Alan Jones, who finished second but did not show up on the podium.

The Argentinian Grand Prix was the last until 1995, Mclaren entered a car with a chassis made from carbon fibre composites. Alan Prost scored his first Formula 1 podium.

Team Lotus withdrew from the San Marino Grand Prix because the FIA upheld the ban on the Lotus 88 with the Twin chassis. It was the first San Marino Grand Prix and the second Formula 1 race held at Imola. The Toleman team made it's Formula 1 debut, Michele Alboreto made his Formula 1 debut at the race.

At the Belgian Grand Prix Osella team mechanic Giovanni Amadeo stumbled off the pitwall into the path of Carlos Reutemann during Friday practice. Amadeo suffered a fractured skull and died from his injuries on the Monday after the race. The mechanics staged a protest over safety before the start of the race and many drivers joined them. The race organisers flagged the warmup lap at the normal time leaving several cars on the grid, this resulted in chaos when the grid reformed at the end of the warm up lap. Nelson Piquet missed his starting position and was sent round on another lap, the rest of the cars were held in position.
The cars began to overrheat and several drivers turned their engines off expecting another formation lap due to Piquet's error, but the organisers began the start sequence once Piquet regained his position. Riccardo Patrese was unable to restart his car and waved his arms to signal he could not start the car, his mechanic Dave Luckett came onto the track to restart the car from behind. As he did so the clerk of the course had already started the lighting sequence to start the race despite the mechanics presence on the starting grid the race started. Siegfried Stohr ploughed into the back of Patrese, hitting the mechanic Luckett who suffered a broken leg and lacerations, the race continued with Stohr's disabled car still on the track. Some furious marshals jumped onto the track and frantically waved at the drivers to stop, the confused drivers waved back at the marshals but on the next lap the drivers stopped. A new rule was introduced that forbade mechanics from being on the grid within 15 seconds of the formation lap and the race starters would use greater caution.
The race was stopped on lap 55 of 70 due to rain.
Alan Jones crashed into the barriers and badly burnt his left thigh after the gearbox oil leaked into the cockpit. It was the last Formula 1 win for Carlos Reutemann and the last Formula 1 win for an Argentinian driver. Nigel Mansell scored his first Formula 1 podium finish.

Nine cars had to pre-qualify for the Monaco Grand Prix, the fastest four went into the main qualifying session. Slim Borgudd, Derek Daly, Eliseo Salazar, Brian Henton and Derek Warwick all failed to pre-qualify. The start of the race was delayed due to a fire at the Hotel Kitchens, water had seeped into the tunnel. Gilles Villenuve won the race, it was his first win since 1979, he became the first and only Canadian to win the Monaco Grand Prix and the only driver from North America to win a Monaco Grand Prix.

The John Player Special sponsorship livery returned to Team Lotus at the Spanish Grand Prix. The race was the last Spanish Grand Prix held at Jarama and the last Spanish Grand Prix until 1986. Gilles Villenuve won the race, it was his final Formula 1 win. Eliseo Salszar had left the March team to join Ensign to replace Marc Surer.

Alain Prost's first Formula 1 win came at the French Grand Prix driving for French team Renault. The race was stopped after 58 laps due to heavy rain, it was later restarted, aggregate times determined the final positions. Jean-Pierre Jabouille retired from Formula 1 before the race, he had not fully recovered from the injuries he had sustained at the 1980 Canadian Grand Prix. Patrick Tambay moved from Theodore Racing to Ligier to replace Jabouille.

John Watson won the British Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win in five years and it was Mclarens first win since 1977 Japanese Grnad Prix. It was also the first win for a carbon fibre composite monocoque Formula 1 car.

The Fittipaldi team were not present at the Austrian Grand Prix as they did not have enough engines, they returned for the Dutch Grand Prix but failed to qualify for the race.

The Italian Grand Prix returned to the Monza Circuit. A Toleman qualified and finished a race for the first time, Siegfried Stohr crashed during qualifying, he decided to retire from racing and start a motor racing academy.

Jacques-Joseph Villenuve made his Formula 1 debut at the Canadian Grand Prix driving for Arrows, he is the brother of Gilles Villenuve. The race was ended on lap 63 of 70 due to the two hour time limit for the races, it was the last time the Canadian Grand prix was held in the autumn. Jacques Laffites won the race, it was his last Formula 1 win and the last win for Ligier for 15 years until the 1996 Monaco Grand Prix

Alan Jones Caesars Palace Grand Prix win was his last win and the last Formula 1 win for an Australian driver until Mark Webber in 2009. The Caesars Palace Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race for Hector Rebaque, the last for a Mexican until Sergio Perez in 2011, Carlos Reutemann scored his last pole position, the last for an Argentinian driver. Carlos Reutemann, Nelson Piquet and Jacques Laffites were all in contention for the drivers title going into the race, Piquet had to finish in the top five and ahead of Reutemann to win the title, which he managed to do.

Drivers who failed to qualify during the season were Miguel-Angel Guerra, Eliseo Salazar, Siegfried Stohr, Kevin Cogan, Derek Daly, Jans Lammers, Beppe Gabbiani, Jean-Pierre Jabouille, Siegfred Stohr, Chico Serra, Derek Warwick, Brian Henton, Rene Arnoux, Slim Borgudd, Patrick Tambay, Keke Rosberg, Hector Rebaque, Piercarlo Ghinzani, Michele Alboreto, Giorgio Francia, Nigel Mansell, Eddie Cheever, Jacques-Joseph Villenuve

1982 Formula 1 Season

Keke Rosberg won the drivers World Championship and Ferrari won the Constructors Championship.

Keke Rosberg only won 1 race during the season, 11 different drivers from 7 different teams won a race during the season. No driver won more than twice, with nine different winners in nine consecutive races from Monaco Grand Prix to the Swiss Grand Prix.

The Argentine Grand Prix was cancelled due to a lack of sponsors and the Spanish Grand Prix was omitted. The Detroit and Swiss Grand Prix were added to the calendar, the United States hosted three Grand Prix during the season, it was the only time a country held three races until 2020.

Alan Jones and Carlos Reutemann had retired from Formula 1 at the end of the 1981 season, but Reutemann returned for two races during the 1982 season. Keke Rosberg replaced Alan Jones at Williams and Niki Lauda returned to Formula 1 after a two year absence. Marc Surer was meant to drive for the Arrows team but he broke both feet in a pre-season test, Patrick Tambay replaced Surer but was fired after taking part in the drivers strike and the seat was eventually filled by Brian Henton.

34 cars were permitted to enter races with 24 cars starting the race, so pre-qualifying was introduced in which the three teams who finished at the bottom of the standings and new teams had to pre-qualify for races.

Before the beginning of the season the drivers had recieved a letter from the FISA containing an application form for their new super licence. The document stated that the drivers would not be allowed to switch team freely during the season, with their licence being withdrawn should they do so. Six drivers refused to sign the document, the drivers who had not signed were excluded from practice at the South African Grand Prix.

Carlos Reutemann scored his final podium finish at the South African Grand Prix, it was the final Formula 1 podium fininsh for an Argentinian driver.

Nelson Piquet crossed the finish line first at the Brazilian Grand Prix and Keke Rosberg crossed in second place. but both were disqualified after their cars were found to be underweight. As a result the FOCA teams boycotted the Sn Marino Grand Prix. Alain Prost was the winner of the race and John Watson finished second and Nigel Mansell third. Many of the drivers suffered from exhaustion due to the intensely hot weather. Carlos Reutemann retired from Formula 1 after the race.

Williams called on Mario Andretti for the United States Grand Prix to replace the retired Carlos Reutemann. The San Marino Grand Prix was boycotted by many teams, only seven teams took part giving a field of just 14 cars. After Nelson Piquet and Keke Rosberg were excluded from the Brazilian Grand Prix due to underweight cars, the FOCA protested and boycotted the race. The FOCA aligned teams that did not boycott the race were Tyrrell, Osella, ATS and Toleman, they cited sponsor obligtions. They joined the FISA aligned Ferrari, Renault and Alfa Romeo for the race.

Gilles Villenuve was killed during qualifying for the Belgian Grand Prix. Jochen Mass was travelling slowly, he saw Villenuve approaching at high speed and moved to the right to let him through on the racing line. At the same instant Villenuve moved right to pass Mass, Gilles Villenuve launched into the air at 200-225 km/h after hitting Mass. He was airbourne for over 100m before nose diving into the ground and disintegrating as it somersaulted along the edge of the track. Villenuve was thrown out into the catch fencing. The first doctor arrived on the scene within 35 seconds to find Villenuve not breathing, he was transported to hospital and diagnosed with a fractured neck and died that evening. Ferrari withdrew from the race.

Eddie Cheever scored his first Formula 1 podium finish at the Belgian Grand Prix, the Fittipaldi team scored their last Formula 1 points.

Ferrari entered only one car for the Monaco Grand Prix, Didier Pironi. Riccardo Patrese won the race, it was his first Formula 1 win.

The Detroit Grand Prix was hels on a temporary Street Circuit.

The Canadian Grand Prix had moved to the summer rather than being held in the autumn. Riccardo Paletti died during his second Formula 1 race, Didier Pironi had stalled on the grid, he signalled he had a problem just as the lights switched to green, it was too late to abort the start. Raul Boesel clipped the back of Pironi spinning into the path of Eliseo Salazar and Jochen Mass, Riccardo Paletti could not react in time and slammed into Patrese at 180km/h catapulting him into path of Geoff Lees. Paletti sustained heavy chest injuries, as the doctor tried to rescue Paletti the fuel tank ignited, Paletti did not have a pulse when the fire was extinguished. He was flown to hospital but died soon after arriving in hospital

Patrick Tambay replaced the late Gilles Villenuve at Ferrari from the Dutch Grand Prix, Didier Pironi won the race, it was his final Formula 1 win.

Jochen Mass immediately retired from Formula 1 after the French Grand Prix, on lap 11 of the race Mass and Mauro Baldi collided. Mass's car went through the catch fencing into the tyre walls and was catapulted into the spectaor area and the car caught fire. Didier Pironi was seriously injured during qualifying for the German Grand Prix, but he did manage to secure pole position, he never raced in Formual 1 again. Pironi was too injured to start the race and Ferrari did not replace him so pole position was left empty for the start of the race.

Nelson Piquet became the first driver in modern Formula 1 to make a planned pit stop when he pitted mid-race during the Austrian Grand Prix, he pitted for fuel and change of tyres. Elio de Angelis won the race, the last win for Lotus until 1985.

Keke Rosberg's only win of the season came at the Swiss Grand Prix, it was his first Forumla one win and the first for a Finnish driver. The race was the first Swiss Grand Prix since 1954 and also the last.

Mario Andretti finished third at the Italian Grand Prix, he also scored pole position for the race. It was his last pole, podium and points in Formula 1. Rene Arnoux announced he would be replacing Andretti at Ferrari.

Tyrrell's first Formula 1 win since 1978 came at the Caesars Palace Grand Prix, Michele Alboreto won the race, he was the 11th different driver to win a race during the season, Tyrrell was the 7th different winning constructor during the season. Keke Rosberg secured the drivers title and Ferrari won the constructors title at the Caesars Palace Grand Prix. It was the last Formula 1 race held in Las Vegas, Ensign and the Fittipaldi teams left Formula 1 and Matra left Formula 1 as an engine supplier and it was the last Formula 1 race for the March team until 1987. The Caesars Palace Grand Prix was the third Formula 1 race held in the United States during the season.

Drivers who failed to pre-qualify during the season were Teo Fabi, Riccardo Paletti, Raul Boesel, Chico Serra, Emilio de Villota

Drivers who failed to qualify during the season were Mauro Baldi, Jan Lammers, Jochen Mass, Derek Warwick, Jean-Pierre Jarier, Roberto Guerreo

1983 Formula 1 Season

Nelson Piquet won the drivers championship and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

Nelson Piquet won his second world championship, he became the last Brabham driver to win the championship.

The March team united with RAM Racing becoming RAM March, the Ensign team was absorbed by Theodore Racing. There was a complete ban on ground effecct and becamae mandatory for the cars to have a flat undertray. Benetton entered Formula 1 as a sponsor of the Tyrrell team.

Thierry Boutsen and Johnny Cecotto made their Formula 1 debuts.

Andrea de Cesaris was excluded from the Brazilian Grand Prix, Keke Rosberg and Elio de Angelis were disqualified. De Cesaris was excluded from the race after failing to stop for the weight check. Elio de Angelis was disqualified for an illegal car change and Keke Rosberg was disqualified for a push start in the pits, he fininshed second but was disqualified. The drivers behind him were not promoted up the order so no one was awarded second place at the Brazilian Grand Prix.

Johnny Cecotto scored his only Formula 1 points at the United States Grand Prix West, it was the last time a Venvuelan driver scored points until 2011. Patrick Tambay won the San Marino Grand Prix driving for Ferrari, it was the last time Ferrari won at Imola until 1999. All the drivers on the podium were French, it was the last race where all the drivers on the podium were from the same Nationality.

Five drivers had to pre-qualify for the Monaco Grand Prix, the drivers were from Toleman, Theodore and RAM teams, both Mclarens failed to qualify for the race during the main qualifying session. It was the last time both Mclaren drivers failed to qualify for a race and it was the only time Niki Lauda failed to qualify. Keke Rosberg won the race, thirty years later his son Nico won the 2013 Monaco Grand Prix, they were the first father and son to win the Formula 1 Monaco Grand Prix.

The Belgian Grand Prix was held at Spa-Francorchamps for the first time since 1970, Thierry Boutsen made his Formula 1 debut.

Tyrrell score their last Formula 1 win at the Detroit Grand Prix. The Spirit Team made their debut at the British Grand Prix and driver Stefan Johansson qualified 14th ahead of both Mclarens.

Alain Prost won the Austrian Grand Prix, it was the final Formula 1 win for the origianl Factory Renault team.

A Grand Prix was meant to be held at Flushing Meadows, but it was cancelled at short notice due to local protests, so the race switched to Brands Hatch as the European Grand Prix. Williams entered a third car for the race for Formula 2 Champion Jonathan Palmer.

Ferrari won the constructors title at the South African Grand Prix, they went into the race battling Renault for the constructors title, Nelson Piquet was fighting for the drivers title with Alain Prost and Rene Arnoux. Piquet fininshed third in the race and clinched the title. It was the last win for Riccardo Patrese until 1990 and it was the last Formula 1 race for Jean-Pierre Jarier.

1984 Formula 1 Season

Niki Lauda won the drivers title and Mclaren won the constructors title.

Niki Lauda won the championship by the smallest margin in Formula 1, just half a point. It was Lauda's third title and his third since 1977.

Mclaren won their first title since 1974.

Teo Fabi completed in the Indianapolis 500, he was the last Formula 1 driver to compete in Formula 1 and Indianapolis 500 in the same season until Fernando Alonso in 2017. Both Teo and Carrado Fabi shared the same Brabham seat allowing Teo to honour his commitments in the CART World Series.

Alain Prost re-joined Mclaren after being sacked by Renault, Patrick Tambay and Derek Warwick drove for Renault and Johnny Cecotto and Ayrton Senna were signed by Toleman. Martin Brundle and Stefan Bellof made their Formula 1 debuts. Ferrari signed Michele Alboreto, their first Italian driver since 1973.

Euro Racing lost its major sponsor Marlboro, their livery changed from red and white to the green and red of Benetton. Riccardo Patrese and Eddie Cheever were signed.

Martin Brundle finished fifth at the Brazilian Grand Prix, but later during the season the Tyrrell team and drivers were disqualified from the season for technical infringement.

Nelson Piquet's first pole position was at the South African Grand Prix, Derek Warwick scored his first Formula 1 podium. Michele Alboreto secured pole position at the Belgian Grand Prix and won the race, he was the first Italian to win for Ferrari since Ludovico Scarfiotti in 1966.

The French Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race to be held at the Dijon Circuit, Andrea de Cesaris failed to qualify for the race so Ligier withdrew Francois Hesnault who had qualified 14th, de Cesaris was allowed to start in 26th place on the grid. Niki Lauda won the race, it was his first win in France since 1975.

Martin Brundle crashed during practice for the Monaco Grand Prix, he crashed heavily at Tabac and landed upside down. He was slightly injured, the Formula 1 doctor concluded that Brundle was slightly concussed and he was not allowed to return to the track. Brundle sat out of the rest of the weekend, the race was delayed for 45 minutes due to heavy rain and the tunnel had to be watered as it was the only dry part of the track. On lap 32 the race was red flagged due to the conditions being so poor.

Patrick Tambay had to withdraw from the Canadian Grand Prix due to injury, after the race Nelson Piquet collapsed due to a badly burnt right foot caused by extreme heat from the Brabham's new nose-mounted oil cooler which burnt a hole in his boot.

Martin Brundle fininshed second at the Detroit Grand Prix, but after the podium ceremony officials had found impurities in the water injection system on Brundles Tyrrell and lead balls were found in the rubber bag containing the water. Samples were sent for analysis and found to contain levels of hydrocarbons. The Tyrrell team were accused of refuelling the car during the race which was banned, the team was disqualified and banned from the remainder of the season and the lost the points won so far. The Tyrrell drivers were allowed to race but were unable to score points.

The track of the Dallas Grand Prix was damaged by the Can-Am race held during the race weekend, the track started breaking up badly so emergency repairs took place all night. The broken up asphalt was replaced with quick drying cement. For the first time since the 1978 Dutch Grand Prix both Lotus drivers qualified on the front row of the grid.

Prior to the British Grand Prix it was announced that the Tyrrell team would be disqualified from the championship for the illegal use of fuel and balllast on their cars. Johnny Cecotto crashed heavily during qualifying and broke both his legs, this ended his Formula 1 career.

The original Renault teams last podium finish was at the German Grand Prix. The Austrian Grand Prix was the 400th Formula 1 race, Austrian Gerhard Berger made his Formula 1 debut at the race.

Mclaren secured their first constructors title since 1974, at the Dutch Grand Prix. There were many rumours circulating at the race, one of the rumours was that Jacques Laffite was leaving Williams to return to Ligier. There was also a rumour the Ayrton Senna would break his contract with the Toleman team and join Lotus, when the move was announced two days after the Dutch Grand Prix the Toleman management was angered as Senna had not informed them. As a result Senna was suspended from the next race, the Italian Grand Prix.

Ayrton Senna was replaced by Pierluigi Martini at the Italian Grand Prix, the FIA court of Appeal upheld the exclusion of the Tyrrell team from the championship, the team was banned from the final three races.

The first Portuguese Grand Prix was held since 1960, Niki Lauda secured the drivers championship.

Drivers who failed to qualify during the season were Jonathan Palmer, Piercarlo Ghinzani, Ayrton Senna, Andrea de Cesaris, Huub Rothengutter, Martin Brundle

1985 Formula 1 Season

Alain Prost won the drivers championship and Mclaren won the constructors championship.

It was the last Formula 1 season for Niki Lauda and also for the original Renault Factory team.

The Australian Grand Prix was added to the calendar and the Belgian Grand Prix returned to Spa-Francorchamps, the Dutch Grand Prix was the last until 2021 and South African GRnad Prix was the last until 1992.

Stefan Bellof and Manfred Winkelhock died during the season at World Endurance Championship races.

Rene Arnoux was sacked by Ferrari after the Brazilian Grand Prix and he was replaced by Stefan Johansson. Ayrton Senna's first Formula 1 win came at the Portuguese Grand Prix.

The Detroit Grand Prix was the only Formula 1 race held in the United States, it was the first time in 10 years that only one Formula 1 race was held in the country during the season. The track was poor and broke up as the race went on.

Nelson Piquet scored the Brabham team's last Formula 1 win when he won the French Grand Prix, it was the last Formula 1 race on the full Paul Ricard Circuit until 2018.

Onboard cameras were used at the German Grand Prix, one was attached to Francois Hesnault's car, he was not eligible for championship points. The race was the first Formula 1 race on the new GP-Strecke section of the Nurburgring that replaced the old Sudschleife section. It was the first time in nine years that the German Grand Prix was held at the Nurburgring. Teo Fabi scored his first pole position, the only ploe for the Toleman team. Michele Alboreto won the race, it was his last Formula 1 win, it was Manfred Winkelhock's final Formula 1 Grand Prix, he was killed during the 1000km of Mosport in Canada.

Kenny Acheson replaced Manfred Winkelhock at the Austrian Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 race since 1983. Niki Lauda announced he would retire at the end of the season. The race was restarted after after one lap due to a crash on the starting grid, on lap 13 Andrea de Cesaris survived a massive crash, his car went sideways at a left hand curve and slid across the grass at high speed. The right rear wheel hit a slight bank launching the car into a serries of rolls, as soon as the car came to rest de Cesaris climbed out. After the race team boss Guy Ligier fired Andrea de Cesaris, who holds the record for the most starts without a win after 208 Formula 1 races.

Niki Lauda's last Formula 1 win was at the Dutch Grand Prix, Stefan Bellof died a week after the race during the 1000km of Spa. The Dutch Grand Prix was the last until 2021.

Alan Jones returned to Formula 1 at the Italian Grand Prix, the Haas Lola team made their Formula 1 debut at the race.

The Spa circuit was damaged during Friday practice, overnight the track was repaired, but during Saturday practice the track was still in a poor condition and the damage was noticed and the session was stopped. The race was posponed for later in the season, when the race went ahead Christian Danner made his Formula 1 debut with Zakspeed and Minardi fininshed a race for the first time.

Alain Prost won the drivers championship at the European Grand Prix, Nigel Mansell won the race, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first win for a British driver since James Hunt in 1977. The race was the last held under the name European Grand Prix until 1993.

The South African Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race held on a Saturday and the last where Laurel wreaths were given to drivers on the podium. The race was also the last South African Grand Prix until 1992. Ligier and Renault boycotted the race and multiple sponsors ordered teams to remove their branding from cars.

The first Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix was held, Mclaren won the constructors title at the race. Alan Jones was the only Australian to take part in the race.

1986 Formula 1 Season

Alain Prost won the drivers championship Williams won the constructors championship.

40th Season of Formula 1.

Fuel was limited to 195 litres during a race which led to many drivers running out of fuel during races. There was a big change to the Formula 1 calendar, the Dutch, South African and European Grand Prix were dropped. The Mexican and Spanish Grand Prix returned and a new Grand Prix was added, the Hungarian Grand Prix. The Mexican Grand Prix returned to the calendar for the first time since 1970 and the Spanish Grand Prix was the first since 1981.

Johnny Dumfries made his Formula 1 debut, it was his only season in Fomrula 1. He raced under the names Jonhhy Dumfries, he full name was John Colum Crichton-Stuart, 7th Marquess of Bute. He won the 24 Hours of Le Mans in 1988.

Jacques Laffite and Marc Surer had their Formula 1 careers ended during the season, Elio de Angelis died during a testing accident at Circuit Paul Ricard. Alan Jones, Keke Rosberg and Patrick Tambay retired at the end of the season, it was also the last Formula 1 season for Dutch driver Huub Rothengutter.

Frank Williams was involved in a serious car accident before the start of the season leaving hima quadriplegic.

The First Spanish Grand Prix was held since 1981, Ayrton Senna won the race by 0.014 seconds, the closest ever Formula 1 race win. Team Lotus started their 350th Formula 1 race and Keke Rosberg made his 100th Formula 1 start, Aryton Senna scored Team Lotus 100th pole position. Williams 150th Formula 1 start came at the San Marino Grand Prix.

Elio de Angelis died four days after the Monaco Grand Prix during a test at the Circuit Paul Ricard.

Ferrari started their 400th Formula 1 race at the French Grand Prix

Jacques Laffite Formula 1 career was ended at the British Grand Prix, at the start of the race there was a multi-car pile up, Laffite broke both his legs. Jonathan Palmer assisted Laffite track side, as before his racing career Palmer qualified as a doctor, Laffite was airlifted to hospital and the race was restarted. The race was the last Formula 1 race held at the Brands Hatch Circuit. Frank Williams returned to the paddock for the first time since his accident four months earlier.

At the German Grand Prix Keke Rosberg announced he would be retiring from Formula 1 at the end of the season. There was an all Mclaren front row at the race, the first time since 1972 Canadian Grand Prix.

The Hungarian Grand Prix was the first Formula 1 race to be held behind the Iron Curtain, it was the first Hungarian Grand Prix to be held in 50 years and the first Formula 1 Hungarian Grand Prix.

Gerhard Berger's first Formula 1 win came at the Mexican Grand Prix and the first win for the Benetton team.

Nigel Mansell, Alain Prost and Nelson Piquet went into the Australian Grand Prix fighting for the championship, it was the last time until the 2007 Brazilian Grand Prix that three drivers went into the final race of the season fighting for the championship. It was the last Formula 1 race for Renault as an engine supplier until 1989, it was also the last Formula 1 race for Alan Jones, Patrick Tambay, Johnny Dumfries, Allen Berg, Huub Rothengutter and Keke Rosberg.

1987 Formula 1 Season

Nelson Piquet won the drivers championship and Williams won the constructors championship.

The Japanese Grand Prix returned to the calendar and March Engineering returned to Formula 1.

March Engineering returned to Formula 1 at the Brazilian Grand Prix, it was their first race since 1982. There was talk of a drivers boycott at the race due to the new Super licence fees, the drivers who scored points during the previous season would be required to pay more. By Friday practice everyone had paid the required fees. Ligier missed the race due to their engine supplier pulling out.

Japanese driver Satoru Nakajima made his Formula 1 debut at the Brazilian Grand Prix.

Goodyear withdrew all the tryres they had issued for the San Marino Grand Prix after reports of blistering, replacements were flown in. Satoru Nakajima scored points at the San Marino Grand Prix, he was the first Japanese driver to score points in Formula 1. Rene Arnoux and Nelson Piquet did not start the race, Arnoux had suspension failure and Piquet had been injured when he had crashed tearing the rear end and was taken to hospital for checks.

Christian Danner was disqualified and excluded from the race, during practice Michele Alboreto and Danner tangled. Alboreto was thrown in the air and the car caught fire and landed back on the track, Danner was blamed for the accident and was disqualified from the weekend.

Before the Hungarian Grand Prix Ayrton Senna informed Lotus he would be leaving at the end of the season, Lotus almost immediately signed Nelson Piquet to replace him.

The Austrian Grand Prix was the last until 1997, During Friday practice Stefan Johansson hit a deer which died instantly, the front suspension of the car broke on impact. Most of the car was destroyed when it hit the guardrail, Johansson managed to escape with only a headache.

AGS and Coloni entered Formula 1 at the Italian Grand Prix.

Alain Prost eclisped Jackie Stewarts record of 27 wins when he won the Portuguese Grand Prix, his 28th Formula 1 win. On the opening lap there was a multi car collision, Nelson Piquet and Michele Alborteo collided at the start with Derek Warwick, Satoru Nakajima, Martin Brundle, Christian Danner, Philippe Alliot, Rene Arnoux and Adrian Campos. Christian Danner was the only driver unable to restart the race.

The first Japanese Grand Prix was held since 1977 and the first race held at the Suzuka circuit. Gerhard Berger won the race, it was the first win for Ferrari since 1985.

1988 Formula 1 Season

Ayrton Senna won the drivers championship and Mclaren won the constructors championship.

Four new teams entered the championship, they were BMS, Scuderia Italia, Rial and EuroBrun who mainly ran one car each, except EuroBrun which ran two cars, it was Coloni's first full seasson in Formula 1. There were now 31 cars entering races, a maximum of 30 cars were allowed in the qualifying session so pre-qulaifying was re-introduced, it consisted of the cars of the new teams. Pre-qualifying took place on Friday morning, the slowest car missed out on the rest of the weekend.

The Brabham team had withdrawn from the 1988 season.

The Jacarepagua circuit was renamed Autodromo Internacional Nelson Piquet. Julien Bailey and Bernd Schneider were both rookies and failed to qualify for the Brazilian Grand Prix. Julian Bailey was making his Formula 1 debut at the age of 33. Bernd Schneider was the German Formula 3 champion.

Ayrton Senna was disqualified from the Brazilian Grand Prix for an illegal car change, Nicola Larini was excluded from the SanMarino Grand Prix for failing scrutineering. Stefano Modena missed a weight check at the Monaco Grand Prix and was excluded from the race.

Bernd Schneider qualified for the Mexican Grand Prix, Stefano Modena was excluded from the race due to an illegal wing on his car.

During Saturday practice for the Detriot Grand Prix Ivan Capelli crashed breaking both his feet.

The 1988 British Grand Prix was the 4oth anniversary of the Silverstone Circuit, new additions had been added to the circuit including a new press centre complex on an internal ring road, a debris fencing, wide screens and a 600m long hospitality tent. Alessandro Nannini scored his first Formula 1 podium finish at the British Grand Prix, the race was the first wet race since 1985 Belgian Grand Prix. Nigel Mansell announced he would race for Ferrari in 1989.

Bernd Schneider qualified for the German Grand Prix and finished the race, it was his first race finish and the highest finish of the season for Zakspeed, he finished 12th. Williams announced that Thierry Boutsen would replace Nigel Mansell in 1989.

Benetton drivers Thierry Boutsen and Alessandro Nannini were disqualified from the Belgian Grand Prix for irregular fuel.

Mclaren won the constructors title at the Belgian Grand Prix with five races remaining.

Benetton announced at the Spanish Grand Prix that Johnny Herbert would replace Thierry Boutsen for the 1989 season.

Ayrton Senna clinched the World Championship at the Japanese Grand Prix.

Aguri Suzuki made his Formula 1 debut at the Japanese Grand Prix, he replaced Yannick Dalmas who was unfit to race.

The original Team Lotus scored their final podium fininsh at the Australian Grand Prix.

Drivers who failed to pre-qualify during the season were Alex Caffi, Gabriele Tarquini, Nicola Larini and Oscar Larrauri.

1989 Formula 1 Season

Alain Prost won the championship andMclaren won the constructors title.

21 constructors entered the championship bringing the number of cars to 40, before the season began FIRST Racing withdrew leaving 20 constructors and 39 cars. It was the highest number of entrants in the modern era. FIRST Racing had failed the mandatory FIA pre-season crash test.

The Brabham team had returned to Formula 1, their drivers Martin Brundle and Stefano Modena had to pre-quaulify along with Zakspeed drivers Bernd Schneider and Aguri Suki. The Onyx, Osella and EuroBrun teams also had to pre qualify along with the second car from Rial, Dallara, AGS and Coloni which had expanded from one car. Onyx Grand Prix were the only new team to enter Formula 1, they had had success in Formula 2 and Formula 3000.

Johnny Herbert and Jean Alesi made their Formula 1 debuts, Herbert joined Benetton and Alesi joined Tyrrell. Bertrand Gachot also made his Formula 1 debut.

During testing Philippe Streiff crashed heavily, he suffered spinal injuries which left him a quadriplegic, Gabriele Tarquini took Streiff's place at AGS.

The Brazilian Grand Prix was the last to be held at the Jacarepagua circuit.

The San Marino Grand prix was red flagged after Gertrand Berger crashed at 180 mph and the car burst into flames, the fire was put out 10 seconds later. Berger escaped with brokken ribs and second degree burns. Half an hour later the race was restarted, Stefano Modena crashed violently into the wall, he escaped unhurt.

Stefano Modeno scored his first Formula 1 points and podium finish and Brabham scored their last Formula 1 podium finish at the Monaco Grand Prix. Gabriele Tarquini only Formula 1 points were scored at the Mexican Grand Prix.

Thierry Boutsen won the Canadian Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win. The first start to the race was aborted after Gerhard Berger stalled on the grid, Nigel Mansell, Alessandro Nannini and Luis Perez-Sala elected to pit for slick tyres at the end of the second formation lap and had to start from the pit lane. Mansell and Nannini left the pit lane to rejoin the circuit 17 seconds before the starting procedure had been completed and the green lights were shown to the rest of the field. Nannini and Mansell were disqualified for starting the race before the grid formation.

Both the Dallara cars finished in the points at the Canadian Grand Prix, it was the last podium finish for De Cesaris and last points for Rene Arnoux.

Alain Prost announced at the French Grand prix that he was leaving Mclaren at the end of the season.

Jean Alesi replaced Michele Alboreto at Tyrrell when the team took on Camel as its major sponsor, this new sponsorship clashed with Alboreto's Marlboro sponsorship. Eric Bernard replaced Yannick Dalmas at Larrouse who was ill, Lotus test driver Martin Donnelly replaced Derek Warwick at Arrows.

Drivers who failed to pre qualify during the season were Alex Caffi, Piercarlo Ghinzani, Volker Weidler, Pierre-Henri Raphanel, Joachim Winkelhock, Aguri Suzuki, Stefan Johansson, Bertrand Gachot, Gregor Foitek, Bernd Schneider, Nicola Larini and Martin Brundle

Drivers who failed to qualify during the season were Yannick Dalmas, Rene Arnoux, Gregor Foitek, Roberto Moreno, Michele Alborteo, Christian Danner, Satoru Nakajima, Luis Perez-Sala, Mauricio Gugelmin, Olivier Grouillard, Johnny Herbert and Andrea de Cesaris.
