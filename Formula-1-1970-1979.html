1970 Formula 1 Season

Jochen Rindt won the championship and Team Lotus won the constructors title.

The Lotus cars featured torsion bar, suspension, hip mounted radiators, inboard front brakes and a overhanging rear wing. Slick tyres were introfuced to Formula 1 by Goodyear. Jack Brabham retired at the end of the season. There were three driver fatalities during the season, Bruce Mclaren died while testing his Can-Am car at the Goodwood Circuit. Piers Courage and Jochen Rindt bothdied during Formula 1 weekends, Piers Courage died during the Dutch Grand Prix and Jochen Rindt died during the Italain Grand Prix weekend. The Italain Grand Prix was four races before the end of the season, Jochen Rindt had scored enought points to be awarded the championship.

Jochen Rindt became the only driver to be posthumously awarded the Formula 1 World Championship.

Graham Hill returned to Formula 1 racing at the South African Grand Prix.

The organisers of the Spanish Grand Prix sparked anger amongst members of the FOCA when they limited the number of starters to 16. Mario Andretti scored his first Formula 1 podium, Bruce Mclaren score his final podium finish. During the race Jackie Oliver and Jacky Ickx crashed, both cars burst into flames. Ickx was slightly burnt after his overalls became covered in burning fuel.

Bruce Mclaren's last Formula 1 race was the Monaco Grand Prix, he died five days later while testing his Can-Am car. Ronnie Peterson made his Formula 1 debut at the race. Pedro Rodriguez won the Belgian Grand prix, it was his last Formula 1 win and the first win for the BRM team since 1966.

Lotus debuted their monocoque-chassis at the Dutch Grand Prix and Clay Regazzoni and Peter Gethin made their Formula 1 debut at the race.

The French Grand Prix was the final Formula 1 race to be held on public roads with no Armco barriers lining the circuit.

Emerson Fittipaldi made his Formula 1 debut at the British Grand Prix which was the final Formula 1 race for Dan Gurney. The German Grand Prix had moved to the Hockenheimring which was fitted with Armco Barriers. It was the last win and podium for Jochem Rindt.

The Austrian Grand Prix was held at the Osterreichring for the first time, Clay Regazzoni scored his first Formula 1 podium finish at the race. He won the following race which was the Itallian Grand Prix, during the practice session Jochen Rindt was killed.

Formula 2 team Tyrrell Racing made their Formula 1 debut at the Canadian Grand Prix. At the United States Grand Prix Jochen Rindt was awarded the World Championship posthumously. It was the only time a driver was posthumously awarded the championship. Brazilian Emerson Fittipaldi won the race, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first for a Brazilian driver. Reine Wisell scored his first and only Formula 1 podium on his debut at the United States Grand Prix.

The 200,000 crowd at the Mexican Grand Prix were almost uncontrollable and some ran across the track. Due to the crowds antics the Mexican Grand Prix was dropped until 1986.

1971 Formula 1 Season

Jackie Stewart won the drivers championship and Tyrrell won the constructors championship.

At least 22 cars started every championship race except at the Monaco Grand Prix where the starting grid was 18. The Maximum race distance was reduced to 325km. Pedro Rodriguez and Jo Siffert died during the season, Rodriguez died during a race at the Norisring and Siffert died during a race at Brands Hatch

Mario Andretti's first Formula 1 win came at the South African Grand Prix.

Goodyear introduced slick tyres at the Spanish Grand Prix. The Monaco Grand Prix was the 200th Formula 1 championship race, the race was filmed for the documentary 'Weekend of a Champion'.

The Zandvoort circuit did not have any safety features, the Dutch Grand Prix was the last with no safety features at the circuit. Pedro Rodriguez was the last Mexican driver to finish on the podium until 2012. It was the last Formula 1 Grand Prix to have no Brazilian driver starting the race until 2017.

The Paul Ricard Circuit held the French Grand Prix for the first time, it was the last Formula 1 race for Pedro Rodriguez who died a week after the race during a race at the Norisring. There was a collision betwwen Jackie Oliver and Graham Hill at the British Grand Prix due to a bungled flag drop.

The German Grand Prix returned to the Nurburgring. Mike Beuttler was disqualified from the race after entering the pits via a short chute after suffering a flat tyre just after the pits. Jo Siffert was also disqualified due to taking the short chute to the pits.

Niki Lauda made his Formula 1 debut at the Austrian Grand Prix, Jackie Stewart won the drivers championship at the race.

The Italain Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race held on a chicane-less Monza circuit, the race was won by an exctremely small margin of 0.01 seconds, the top five were covered by just 0.61 seconds. Peter Gethin won the race, it was his only Formula 1 win and the last for an English man until James Hunt in 1975. Tyrrell won the constructors championship. There was an ongoing legal issues between Team Lotus and the Italian authorities following Jochen Rindt's death the year before. The team was entered under World Wide Racing.

On his Formula 1 debut Mark Donahue scored a podium finish at the Canadian Grand Prix, the race was stopped after 64 laps of scheduled 80 due to poor weather. It was red flagged, the first ever Formula 1 race to be red flagged.

The United States Grand Prix was the final Formula 1 race for Jo Siffert and Jo Bonnier.

1972 Formula 1 Season

Emerson Fittipaldi won the drivers title and Team Lotus won the constructors championship. Emerson Fittipaldi became the youngest Formula 1 World Champion.

Team Lotus had a new livery, the black and gold of the John Player Special brand of Imperial Tobacco. All the circuits now had safety features, the Dutch and Mexican Grand Pix were cancelled due to safety issues.

Argentinian driver Carlos Reutemann scored pole position oh home soil on his debut. Brazilian Carlos Pace scored his first Formula 1 points at the Spanish Grand Prix. Older brother of Emerson Fittipaldi, Wilson, made his Formula 1 debut at the Spanish Grand Prix. They were the first brothers to race together in Formula 1.

The pits at the Monaco Grand Prix had moved to the harbour front between the chicane and Tabac. The race was won by Jean-Pierre Beltoise, it was his only Formula 1 win and the last Formula 1 win for the BRM team.

The French Grand Prix was held at the Circuit de Charade, which was around the base of an extinct volcano. The drivers who skirted the track edge often sent stones flying into the middle of the track into the path of other cars. A stone penetrated the visor of Helmut Marko, it blinded him in his left eye ending his racing career. 10 cars suffered punctures during the race.

Jacky Ickx final Formula 1 win came at the German Grand Prix,

Emerson Fittipaldi became the youngest ever Formula 1 champion at the age of 25 year. He won the championship at the Italian Grand Prix, Fittipaldi held the title of the youngest Formula 1 champion until 2005.

The Canadian Grand Prix was held at the Mosport Park circuit after the Mont Tremblant circuit was closed due to a dispute with the local racing authorities.

1973 Formula 1 Season

Jackie Stewart won the drivers championship, John Player Team Lotus won the constructors championship.

Two new Grand Prix were introduced, the Brazilian Grand Prix at Interlagos and the Swedish Grand Prix at Anderstorp. At the Canadian Grand Prix the safety car was used for the first time and the warm-up lap was introduced where the cars did a full warm-up lap before the race. A numbering system for teams was formalised in Brazil, team mates were paired up in the numbering. The Lotus team were assigned numbers 1 and 2, Tyrrell were given 3 and 4 etc, the numbers assigned were changed at the Belgian Grand Prix when the order was set for the rest of the season. For the 1974 season the numbers were assigned based on the 1973 final standings.

Francois Cevert dided during the United States GRand Prix practice, Jackie Stewart and the Tyrrell team withdrew from the race. Roger Williamson was killed during the Dutch Grand Prix.

Ronnie Peterson won his first Formula 1 pole position at the Brazilian Grand Prix. During the South African Grand Prix Mike Hailwood pulled Clay Regazzoni from his burning car after the two collided onlap 2. Hailwood's driving suit caught fire, after being extinguished by a fire marshal he returned to help Regazzoni. Hailwood was later awarded the George Medal.

The Zolder track had to be resurfaced a week before the Belgian Grand Prix as the drivers had inspected the track and found it had started to break up. The Monaco circuit had been heavily revised, the Gasworks hairpin replaced Rascasse and Antony Naghes corners. The pits were moved back to the start-finish straight on a wide pit lane. Jackie Stewart equalled the 25 Formula 1 wins record set by Jim CLark when he won the Monaco Grand Prix, James Hunt made his Formula 1 debut at the race.

Ronnie Peterson won the French Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win. Rikky Von Opel made his Formula 1 debut at the race, he is the only Formula 1 driver from Liechtenstein. Opel is the great grandson of Adam Opel, the founder of the German car maker Opel.

The British Grand Prix was the only Formula 1 race with four drivers from New Zealand taking part, the drivers were Denny Hulme, Chris Amon, Howden Ganley and Graham McRae. On the first lap there was a pile up, 11 cars retired from the race. Jody Scheckter spun across the track at woodcote causing the pile up. Andrea de Adamich suffered a broken ankle which ended his Formula 1 career, the race was stopped at the end of lap 2 and restarted with 18 cars.

James Hunt scored his first Formula 1 podium finish at the Dutch Grand Prix, Jackie Stewart won the race becoming the most successful driver with 26 wins. On lap 8 Roger Williamson hit the barrier at speed, he was catapulted 275m across the barriers on the other side. The petrol tank ignited and the car caught fire, Williamson had not been seriously injured but was trapped in the car. David Purley stopped on the opposite side of the track and ran across the live track to assist Williamson. Purley tried to upright the car but was unable to do so by himself, the marshals were not wearing flame retardant overalls and were unable to help. Race control assumed Purley's car had crashed and the driver had escaped, the race continued at full pace. There was only a single fire extinguisher in the area which was not enough to put the fire out, a marshal led a distraught Purley away when the situation became helpless. Some spectators tried to breach the safety fences to try to help but were pushed back by security, it took eight minutes for the fire engines to completely travel around the track. Roger Williamson had died of asphyxiation by the time the car was finally uprighted, a blanket was thrown over the burnt out wreck with Williamson still inside, the race carried on. David Purley was awarded the George medal for bravery.

On lap 2 of the German Grand Prix Niki Lauda broke his wrist in a crash. The March team did not participate in the race following Roger Williamson death. Ferrari, Ensign, Tecno and Hesketh did not participate in the race, Mclaren, Brabham and Surtees entered three cars each.

Jackie Stewart scored his final podium finish at the Austrian Grand Prix, Carlos Pace scored his first Formula 1 podium finish. Niki Lauda was forced to miss his home Grand Prix after breaking his wrist during the previous race, the BRM team did not replace him.

Jacki Stewart secured the championship at the Italian Grand Prix.

Peter Revson won the Canadian GRand Prix, it was Jackie Stewart's Formula 1 race. The race started in very wet conditions, Francois Cevert and Jody Scheckter collided on lap 32 and the safety car was deployed for the first time in Formula 1. The small pit lane became very busy, many drivers had to drive throught as there was no room for them. This caused confusion as some believed Howden Ganley wass in the lead and others believed Emerson Fittipaldi was. Emerson Fittipaldi was not show the chequred flag, instead the flag was waved over a group of cars consisting of Howden Ganley, Mike Hailwood, Peter Revson and James Hunt. Ganley did not believe he had won the race but the officials announced Peter Revson as the race winner.

Francois Cevert died during practice for the United States Grand Prix, he crashed in the uphill esses, he ran too high on the kerbs, slid into the right hand guardrail. The car lashed sideways across the track at 150mph at almost 90 degrees, the nose submarined into the ground, the car flipped upwards and over the barriers coming to rest upside down ontop of the Armco barrier and Cevert died instantly. The Tyrrell team withdrew from the race.

Team Lotus won the constructors title at the United States Grand Prix.

1974 Formula 1 Season

Emerson Fittipaldi won the drivers championship and Mclaren won the constructors championship.

Mclaren won their first constructors championship

The teams had permanent racing numbers, Graham Hill ran a new team, Lotus Embassy Racing with Graham Hill. The AAW Racing Team made their Formula 1 debut, they were the first Finnish team to enter Formula 1. Most of the teams changed their driver line ups, only four drivers stayed with their 1973 teams, they were Ronnie Peterson, Denny Hulme, Carlos Reutemann and James Hunt. Marlboro and Texaco became Mclaren's new sponsors, Emerson Fittipaldi moved to Mclaren from Lotus. Ferrari had a new driver line up of Niki Lauda and Clay Regazzoni. Wilson Fittipaldi had left Formula 1 as a driver to start his own racing team.

Denny Hulme won the Argentine Grand Prix, it was his final Formula 1 win and the last Formula 1 win for a New Zealander.

The Interlagos track had to be swept clear of broken glass from the spectators celebrating Emerson Fittipaldi's pole position win, this delayed thee start of the Brazilian Grand Prix. The heavens opened during the race which was red flagged after lap 32.

Carlos Reutemann won the South African Grand Prix, becoming the first Argentinian to win a Formula 1 race since 1957. Also it was the first win for the Brabham team since 1970. Peter Revson died during the test session before the South African Grand Prix, the car sufferd front suspension failure on the outside of a bend and crashed heavily into the Armco barrier and burst into flames. Revson died instantly, the Shadow team withdrew from the race.

Niki Lauda's first Formula 1 win was at the Spanish Grand Prix, Jody Scheckter scored his first Formula 1 points at the race.

The Belgian Grand Prix was the last to be held at the Circuit Nivelles-Baulers, Tom Pryce made his Formula 1 debut at the race.

Jody Scheckter's first Formula 1 win came at the Swedish Grand Prix and it was the first win for a South African driver. Vern Schuppan was disqualified from the race for illegally stating the race, at the following Grand Prix at Zandvoort he was disqualified for recieving a tyre change outside the pit.

Niki Lauda scored the shortest pole position lap time in Formula 1 history by scoring pole position with a time of 58.79 seconds at the French Grand Prix. The race was the only Formula 1 race held on the Dijon Circuits 3.289km track.

Female driver Lella Lombardi made her Formula 1 debut at the British Grand Prix, her race number was #208 because she was sponsored by Radio Luxembourg and 208 was the stations frequency.

The main straight of the Nurburgring had been widened and resurfaced and the bushes lining the straight were replaced with an Armco barrier. During the weekend Mike Hailwood crashed braking his leg, this ended his Formula 1 career, Howden Ganley crashed during practice, the rear suspension failed. Ganley hit the barrier ripping the nose section of the car off and he serriously injured his ankles, this ended his Formula 1 career.

Helmut Koinigg made his Formula 1 debut at the Candaian Grand Prix, but tragicly lost his life at the following race the United States Grand Prix. Mclaren won their first Formula 1 constructors champinship at the United States Grand Prix. Both Tim Schenken and Mario Andretti were disqualified from the race due to entering the race illegally. Tim Schenken to the race start despite not qualifying for the race, Mario Andretti and Mark Donohue were driving American built cars for American teams. Andretti stalled on the grid and recieved a push start, he was disqualified from the race. On lap 10 Helmut Koinigg went off at the hairpin, his car had suffered suspension failure and pitched head-on into the Armco barrier, the speed of the crash was minor but the Armco barrier was insecurely installed and the bottom portion buckled as the car struck it. The car passed underneath the barrier, the top portion remained intact which decapitated Koinigg who was killed instantly. Mclaren and Ferrari went into the United States Grand Prix fighting for the constructors title. It was the final time until 1981 that three drivers went into the final race of the season fighting for the drivers title. Emerson Fittipaldi, Clay Regazzoni and Jody Scheckter went into the race fighting for the championship, Fittipaldi won the championship.

1975 Formula 1 Season

Niki Lauda won the drivers title and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

The Canadian Grand Prix had been cancelled.

Last season for Graham Hill, he took part in the season, after the end of the season he died in a plane crash in late November. American Mark Donohue died two days after he crashed during practice for the Austrian Grand Prix.

Carlos Pace only Formula 1 win came at the Brazilian Grand Prix, his home race. Jody Scheckter won his home Grand Prix, the South African Grand Prix, it was his only home win.

Jochen Mass was the last German driver to win a Formula 1 race until 1992, he won the Spanish Grand Prix. Australian Alan Jones made his debut at the race. Lella Lombardi became the first and only female driver to score points in Formula 1. Four spectators were killed when Rolf Stommelen crashed during the race which was the last to be held at the Montjuic Street Circuit.

Extra guard rails and catch fences were erected at Monaco and the chicane was modified, the grid was staggered and restricted to 18 cars. Niki Lauda's Monaco win broke the twenty year drought at Monaco for Ferrari, John Surtees was ordered to remove the pro-Europe political stickers from his cars.

James Hunt's first Formula 1 was at the Dutch Grand Prix, it was the first win for Hesketh Racing and the first British driver to win a Formula 1 race since Peter Gethin in 1971. British driver Tom Pryce scored his only Formula 1 pole position at the British Grand Prix, Emerson Fittipaldi won the race, it was his last Formula 1 win. The light system was used for the first time to start a race, it replaced the traditional national flag drop to start the race. It's first use was at the British Grand Prix. Graham Hill announced his Formula 1 retirement at the British Grand Prix.

The Austrian Grand Prix was shortened by heavy rain and half points were awarded, Vittorio Brambilla won the race. It was Brambilla's first Formula 1 win and his was 37. Brian Henton, Wilson Fittipaldi and Mark Donohue crashed during practice. Fittipaldi broke bones in his hand and Mark Donohue died. Donohue crashed, the car went through the catch fencing and advertising billboards lining the track, he died along with a track marshal.

Ferrari clinched the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix, it was their first title since 1964.

1976 Formula 1 Season

James Hunt won the drivers championship and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

The race season had extended to 16 rounds, the Argentine Grand Prix at Buenos Aires was cancelled due to economic reasons. Two new races were added to the calendar, the United States Grand Prix West at Long Beach in California and the Japanese Grand Prix at Fuji Speedway.

French team Ligier made their Formula 1 debut, it was the first French constructor since 1972, the Shadow team recieved a British licence becoming the first constructor to officially change Nationality. James Hunt moved from Hesketh to Mclaren to replace Emerson Fittipaldi who had moved to his brothers team Fittipaldi Automotives.

The United States Grand Prix West was the first new race added to the calendar since 1973, the circuit was a temporary street circuit with a concrete surface. The United States became the second country to hold two Formula 1 races in the same year, It was the second Formula 1 race to be held in California, the first was the 1960 United States Grand Prix at the Riverside.

James Hunt won the Spanish Grand Prix but was disqualified in post-race scrutinerring, Mclaren appealed the disqualification and the appeal was upheld and Hunt was reinstated as the winner. New team Equipe Ligier scored their first podium finish at the Belgian Grand Prix, the fifth race of the season. Jody Scheckter won the Swedish Grand Prix in Tyrrell's six wheeled car, it was the only win for a six wheeled car. The four front wheels were meant to increase the mechanical front end grip.

The British Grand Prix was red flagged due a first corner crash which involved James Hunt. He drove his damaged car back to the pits but did not compete the full lap of the track to do so, instead he drove through the access road on the Cooper straight. Hunt was not allowed to take the restart, but the stewards le t him take it fearing crowd trouble if they did not. James Hunt won the race, immediately after the race Ferrari, Tyrrell and Fittipaldi teams protested against Hunt, a decision was reached in September, Hunt was disqualified.

The last Formula 1 race was held on the Nordschleife section of the Nurburgring, it was the longest circuit on the calendar at 14.2 miles with over 300m elevation change. Niki Lauda was not happy with the safety conditions and attempted to arrange a boycott of the race. Other drivers voted against it and the race went ahead. Jacky Ickx had been fired from Walter Wolf Racing before the race and was replaced by Arturo Merzario, Scuderia Rondini made their Formula 1 debut. The RAM cars had been inpounded by the police because of legal action by former driver Loris Kessel, Rolf Stommelen was transfered from RAM to the Brabham Team. During the race Niki Lauda crashed, his car snapped to the right, he spun through the fencing into an earth bank. The car bounced back onto the track eveloped in flames. Harald Ertl and Brett Lunger hit Lauda, the drivers stopped and helped Lauda out, Niki Lauda suffered serious burns and was rushed to hospital. The race was stoppped, when it was restarted only 20 cars restarted, Chris Amon retired from racing after the race but returned later for the Canadian Grand Prix.

The Austrian Grand Prix was the last time an American licensed constructor won a Formula 1 race, John Watson won the race in a Penske car, it was the only Formula 1 win for Watson and Penske team. Otto Stuppacher and Karl Oppitchauser applied to enter the race but were refused due to their lack of experience. Ferrari did not enter the Austrian Grand Prix due reinstatement of James Hunt as the winner of the Spanish Grand Prix.

Niki Lauda returned from injury at the Italian Grand Prix, only two races after his serious crash at the German Grand Prix. John Watson, James Hunt and Jochen Mass qualifying times for the Italian Grand Prix were disallowed due to fuel irregulatrities, their Friday times werre used for their grid positions. Their times were the slowest of the drivers, Brett Lunger, Arturo Merzario and Otto Stuppacher had all failed to qualifying for the race, but due to the disallowed times of Watson, Hunt and Mass, Lunger, Merzario and Stuppacher had not qualified for the race. Otto Stuppacher had already left the circuit and flown home which lead to Hunt being promoted to the grid position. Arturo Merzario withdrew which promoted Mass into a grid position and Guy Edwards withdrew promoting Watson onto the grid.

Otto Stuppacher failed to qualify for the Canadian Grand Prix, Chris Amon and Harald Ertl did not start the race following an accident during practice.

There was heavy rain during the Friday and Saturday of the United States Grand Prix, over night on the Saturday snow fell, but on Sunday the sun came out int time for the race. Jacky Ickx crashed into the Armco barrier head on, the nose went under the bottom rail and the car split in two. The rear section spun back onto the track in flames, Ickx managed to get out of the car and hobbled to the grass where he collapsed. He had injuries to his legs and ankles. Ferrari won the constructors title at the United States GRand Prix.

Japanese driver Noritake replaced Brett Lunger at Team Surtees for the Japanese Grand Prix, Masami Kawashima replaced Warwick Brown at Wolf-Williams, but his sponsorship money failed to appear so Kawashima was replaced by Hans Binder. Japanese team Maki Engineering entered a car for Masahiro Hosemi. Mario Andretti won the race, it was his second Formula 1 win, five years after his first. Both James Hunt and Niki Lauda went into the final race of the season fighting for the drivers title, James Hunt came third in the race and clinched the drivers championship.

1977 Formula 1 Season

Niki Lauda won the drivers championship and Ferrari won the constructors championship.

The calendar expanded to 17 races, the German ATS team took over the Penske cars and Renault enterd Formula 1. The BRM team qualified for the South African GRand Prix, it was the last time the team ever qualified for a Formula 1 race.

Wolf Racing made their Formula 1 debut at the Argentine Grand Prix which was the final podium finish for Carlos Pace.

The South African Grand Prix was a tragic race, on lap 22 of the race Rezo Zarzi pullled off the circuit to the left side of the straight just after the brow of the hill and the bridge over the track. He was having problems with the fuel metering unit which was pumping fuel directly into the engine which caught fire. Zorzi could not disconnect the oxygen pipe from the helmet so couls not get out. The Oxygen pipes were used to prevent drivers being suffocated if they were trapped in a car in a fire. Two marshals from the pit wall on the opposite side of the track intervened, the first marshal, 25 year old William managed to cross the track. Second marshal 19 year old Frederik Jansen Van Vuuren was carrying a fire extinguisher and did not make it across the track in time. Hans-Joachim Stuck and Tom Pryce came over the brow of the rise in the track, Stuck saw Jansen Van Vuuren and moved to the right to avoid the marshals. Pryce could not see the marshall and unable to react in time he struck Jansen Van Vuuren at 270km/h who was thrown into the air and landed in front of Zorzi and died on impact. The fire extinguisher Jansen Van Vuuren was carrying hit Pryce's head before striking the roll hoop, it bounced over the grandstand and alnded in the car park to the rear of the stand and hit a car. The impact of the extinguisher had wrenched Pryce's helmet upwards sharply and death was almost instantaneous, his car continued down the main straight at speed towards the first corner. The car left the track to the right scraping metal barriers hitting the entrance for emergency vehicles, the car veered back onto the track, it then hit Jacques Laffite's car sending both cars head on into the barriers.

Mario Andretti was the first American driver to win a Formula 1 race on home soil when he won the United States Grand Prix West. Lotus debuted their ground-effect car. Alan Jones was signed as the replacement for Tom Pryce and Hans-Joachim Stuck replaced Carlos Pace who had died in an aircraft accident less then two weeks after the South African Grand Prix. Stuck was meant to driver for ATS but went to Brabham instead, ATS signed Jean-Pierre Jarier instead.

At the Spanish Grand Prix 31 cars entered the race but only 25 cars could start the race, six drivers failed to qualify for the race. Riccardo Patrese made his Formula 1 debut at the Monaco Grand Prix.

There were two Swedish driver on the podium at the Belgian Grand Prix, it was the only time it happened. Gunnar Nilsson won the race, it was his only Formula 1 win, Ronnie Peterson finished third.

Jacques Laffite became the first French driver to win a Formula 1 race in a French car of a French team when he won the Swedish Grand Prix.

The British Grand Prix was the last race to be given the honoury title of the European Grand Prix, all following European Grand Prix were title of races rather than an honory title of a race. Gilles Villenuve made his Formula 1 debut at the race and qualified for the race finishing 11th in the race. 40 cars entered the British Grand Prix, it was the largest number of entrants meaning fourteen cars had to pre-qualify. The fastest five drivers from sessions 1 and 2 went into the full qualifying session.

The German Grand Prix moved to Hockenheimring again, Hans Heyer participated in the race despite not qualifying, but a maechanical issue forced him to retire from the race on lap 9. This caused his participation to be discovered and he was disqualified, and became the only driver to not qualify, not finish and be disqualified in a single race.

Ferrari won the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix.

Alan Jones' first Formula 1 win was at the Austrian GRand Prix, it was the only Formula 1 win for the shadow team. Niki Lauda won the drivers championship at the United States Grand Prix, he quit Ferrari after the race as he had announced his intention to moc=ve to Brabham for 1978.

During practice for the Canadian Grand Prix Ian Ashley was seriously injured when his car hit a bump, flipped, vaulted the barrier and crashed into the television tower, the crash ended his Formula 1 career. Later Jochen Mass crashed into a barrier that flatterned upon inpact, he was uninjured and finished the race in third place, it was his final Formula 1 podium. The Canadian Grand Prix was the only race Ferrari failed to win during the season, Jody Scheckter won the race, it was the last race won by a non-European constructor until 2006. It was the last time a Formula 1 race was held at Mosport Park.

The Japanese Grand Prix was the last to be held at Fuji Speedway until 2007 and the last Japanese Grand Prix until 1987. A marshal and photographer were killed by debris following a collision involving Gilles Villenuve and Ronnie Peterson on lap 5, the marshal and photographer had been standing in a prohibited area of the track. After the race concluded James Hunt and Carlos Reutemann left the circuit immediately to catch flights home. Hunt had finished first and Reutemann had finished second, so there was just Patrick Depailler and his engineer on the podium. Rules were changed making it mandatory to attend podium celebrations. Ian Scheckter was expelled from Japan due to only having a tourist visa in his passport and he needed a working visa. Riccardo Patrese scored his first points in Formula 1, it was the final Formula 1 win for James Hunt. Team Lotus had a red livery for the race promoting the parent company Imperial Tobacco instead of their usual John Player special livery.

Drivers who failed to qualify for races were Jean-Pierre Jarier, Alex Ribeiro, Boy Hayje, Brian Henton, David Purley, Conny Andersson, Arturo Merzario, Harald Ertl, Clay Regazzoni, Ian Scheckter, Emilio de Villota, Bernard de Dryver, Larry Perkins, Hector Rebaque, Mikko Kozarowitzky, Patrick Neve, Brett Langer, Patrick Tambay, Hans Heyer, Emerson Fittipaldi, Teddy Pilette, Loris Kessel, Giorgio Francia, Vern Schuppen, Michael Bleekemolen, Jean-Pierre Jabouille.

1978 Formula 1 Season

Mario Andretti won the World Championship and Team Lotus won the constructors championship.

The Japanese Grand Prix was cancelled and the Brazilian and Canadian Grand Prix moved to new circuits.

Mario Andretti was the last American to win the Formula 1 World Championship and Ronnie Peterson was awarded second place in the championship posthumously.

Emerson Fittipaldi finished second at the Brazilian Grand Prix, it was the first podium finish for the Fittipaldi team. The race had moved to the Jacarepagua circuit from the Interlagos ciruit. Keke Rosberg made his Formula 1 debut at the South African Grand Prix.

Brabham debuted their fan car at the Swedish Grand Prix, it was the only race if featured in and the only time the fan car won a race. The other teams protested about the fan car but it was deemed legal, Brabham voluntarily withdrew the car and it was never entered in a race again. Riccardo Patrese scored his first Formula 1 podium finish as the Swedish Grand Prix. Mario Andretti was the last American to win a Formula 1 Grand Prix when he won the Dutch Grand Prix, which was his lat Formula 1 win, it was also the final Formula 1 podium finish for Ronnie Peterson.

Nelson Piquet made his Formula 1 debut at the German Grand Prix, the Rebacque team scored their only points finiah in Formula 1. At the Austrian Grand Prix the race was stopped on lap 4 due to a heavy rainshower, the race was later restarted, Ronnie Peterson won the race, it was the last Formula 1 win for a Swedish driver. Lotus won the constructors championship at the Austrian Grand Prix. The French Grand Prix was James Hunt's final Formula 1 podium.

The Italian Grand Prix starter turned on the red lights before all the cars had lined up, the result was a funnelling effect of cars approaching the chicane and they were bunched together. James Hunt veered left and hit Ronnie Peterson, Vittorio Brambilla, Hans-Joachim Stuck, Patrick Depailler, Didier Pironi, Derek Daly, Clay Regazzoni and Brett Lunger. Peterson went into the barriers on the right-hand side and the car caught fire, Regazzoni and Depailler managed to free Peterson from the wreck. It took 20 minutes before medical help was dispatched to Ronnie Peterson who had severe leg injuries, Vittorio Brambilla had been hit on the head by a wheel which rendered him unconscious. Ronnie Peterson was taken to hospital.
The race was restarted at 5:15pm, Jody Scheckter crashed on his way to the grid bending the barrier, some drivers refused to start the race until the barrier was repaired. The race was finally restarted at 6:15pm and was shortened to 40 laps. During the night Peterson's condition worsened, he was diagnosed with a fat embolism, he had kidney failure by the morning and he was declared deceased on 11 September 1978. Niki Lauda won the Italian Grand Prix, it was his last Formula 1 win until 1982 and the last Formula 1 win for an Alfa Romeo, Mario Andretti won the championship.

Before the United States Grand Prix the Grand Prix Drivers Association (GPDA) had a meeting centering on Riccardo Patrese who they blamed for Ronnie Peterson's crash and Patrese was banned from the race. Jean-Pierre Jabouille scored the first Formula 1 points for Renault and Williams scored their first Formula 1 podium finish. It was the first race that a specially trained medical team lined up behind the cars at the start of the race and followed for most of the first lap, this became standard procedure. The medical car hit a curb and flew into the air.

The Circuit Ile Notre-Dame was on a man made island in the middle of the St Lawrence seaway. Before the race it was announced by Brabham theat Nelson Piquet would be joining them in 1979. Riccardo Patrese returned after his ban, Gilles Villenuve won the Canadian Grand PRix, it was his first Formula 1 win and his first win on home soil.

1979 Formula 1 Season

Jody Scheckter won the championship and Ferrari won the constructors title.

Jody Scheckter was the last Ferrari driver to win the championship until Michael Schumacher in 2000. Carlos Reutemann replaced Ronnie Peterson at Lotus, Ferrari signed Jody Scheckter. James Hunt moved to Wolf Racing, John Watson moved to Mclaren and Nelson Piquet replaced John Watson at Brabham. John Player Special withdrew from Formula 1, Martini, Tissot and Essex Petroleum became the new sponsors of Lotus. Olympus Cameras became the main sponsors of Wolf Racing. Team Surtees and Theodore Racing both left Formula 1, Ligier and Williams entered two cars each. In Sweden enthusiasm for Formula 1 had faded and there were also no funds, so the Swedish Grand Prix was cancelled.

Gilles Villenuve drove past his starting position at the United States Grand Prix, it confused the entire field and everyone ended up back at in the pits before going back to the grid, Renault withdrew from the race due to safety problems. Alfa Romeo returned to Formula 1 as a works team since 1951, their first race was the Belgian Grand Prix.

French driver Jean-Pierre Jabouille won the French Grand Prix driving for a French team and using tyres supplied by a French supplier, the car was powered by a French engine. It was the first Formula 1 win for Renault and Jean-Pierre Jabouille.

The first Formula 1 win for Williams came at the 1979 British Grand Prix, James Hunt had retired from racing at the Monaco Grand Prix and started commentating alongside Murray Walker for the BBC. At the following race the German Grand Prix, Williams had their first 1-2 finish and their second win in a row. Clay Regazzoni had won the British Grand Prix and Alan Jones won the German Grand Prix.

Jody Scheckter clinched the drivers title and Ferrari won the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix.

Niki Lauda announced his retirement from Formula 1 at the Canadian Grand Prix, he was replaced by Argentine Ricardo Zunino at Brabham. The Alfa Romeo team were told they had to pre-qualify if they wanted to take part in the Canadian Grand Prix, they refused. The United States Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race for Jacky Ickx, Alex Ribeiro, Arturo Merzario, Hans-Joachim Stuck and Wolf Racing.
