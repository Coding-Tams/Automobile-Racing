1950 Formula 1 Season

Giuseppe Farina won the championship.

First season of the FIA World Championship of Drivers, the Indianapolis 500 was classed as part of the championship but none of the regular Formula 1 drivers or teams took part. Out of the 14 teams that took part in the championship four were works teams.

The first race was the British Grand Prix at Silverstone, Giuseppe Farina won the race and all the drivers on the podium were Alfa Romeo drivers. Nine of the drivers were from the UK, the Ferrari team did not take part.

There was a large pile up on the first lap of the Monaco Grand Prix due to a wave from the harbour flooding the track at Tabac corner. Louis Chiron finished third in the race, the only driver from Monaco to score points in Formula 1 until Charles Leclerc in 2018. The last race of the season was the Italian Grand Prix, Juan Manuel Fangio, Luigi Fagioli and Giuseppe Farina went into the race with a chance of winning the championship. Giuseppe Farina won his home race and won the championship becoming the first Formula 1 World Champion and the only driver to win the championship on home soil.

1951 Formula 1 Season

Juan Manuel Fangio won the championship.

Strling Moss made his Formula 1 debut at the Swiss Grand Prix.

53 year old Luigi Fagioli became the oldest driver to win a race when he won the French Grand Prix, he shared his car and win with Juan Manuel Fangio. It was Fagioli's first win and it was the first time that two drivers were credited with a Formula 1 win after sharing a car. Luigi Fagioli was ordered to swap cars with Juan Manuel Fangio when Fangio's car was misfiring. After the race Fagioli quit Formula 1 as he was furious that he had to had over his car to his team mate. During the race Jose Froilan Gonzalez had to hand his car the Alberto Ascari when Ascari's gear box broke.

Jose Froilan Gonzalez won the British Grand Prix in an old chassis, his team mate had a newer chassis. It was also Ferrari's first Formula 1 win and pole position, it was the first pole in Formula 1 not held by an Alfa Romeo and first race not won by an Alfa Romeo. British team BRM arrived late to the race and had not practiced or qualified, they started 19th and 20th but were soon chasing the race leaders.

Alberto Ascari's first Formula 1 win came at the German Grand Prix. Jean Behra secretly replaced Maurice Trintigant at the Italian Grand Prix due to Trintigant being ill. The team principal Amedee Gordini did not inform the race organisers about the switch as it would have cut the teams starting fee. Felice Bonetto and Giuseppe Farina finished third at the Italian Grand Prix due to sharing a car, Bonetto handed his car over to Farina on lap 29.

The German and Spanish Grand Prix were new races added to the Formula 1 calendar. Juan Maneul Fangio, Alberto Ascari and Jose Froilan Gonzalez went into the Spanish Grand Prix battling for the title. Juan Manuel Fangio won the race and Jose Froilan Gonzalez finished second, Fangio won the championship.

1952 Formula 1 Season

Alberto Ascari won the championship

Alfa Romeo and BRM withdrew from Formula 1 leaving only Ferrari as a serious contender so all the races were run to Formula 2 regulations. Alberto Ascari took part in the Indianapolis 500, the first European driver to do so/ He missed the Swiss Grand Prix due to qualifying for the event.

The Swiss Grand Prix was the first of the races to be under Formula 2 regulation. Rudi Fischer finished second in the race, the first Swiss driver to finish on the podium. New pit facilities had been built on the straight between Woodcote and Copse at Silverstone, the original pits were between abbey and Woodcote.

Alberto Ascari clinched the World Championship at the German Grand Prix. He won the Dutch Grand Prix and overtook Juan Manuel Fangio's record for the most race wins

1953 Formula 1 Season

Alberto Ascari won the championship, successfully defending his title.

The Argentine Grand Prix was added to the calendar, it was the first Formula 1 race outside of Europe and North America. The Spanish Grand Prix had been cancelled. For a second season the championship was run to Formula 2 regulations.

President Juan Peron decided to allow free access to the Argentine Grand Prix, the spectators lined the track as the race started. One spectator wandered onto the track, Giuseppe Farina was forced to swerve and he lost control and crashed into the crowd on lap 31 killing 13 people. Argentine driver Oscar Alfredo Galvez scored his only Formula 1 points at the race which was his only Formula 1 race. Later the track was named after him due to his successful racing career.

The Belgian Grand Prix win was ALberto Ascari's 9th consecutive win.

Mike Hawthorn won the French Grand Prix, he was the first Britsh driver to win a Formula 1 race.

The German Grand Prix had the largest grid of the season with 34 starters, the largest amount of starters in Formula 1. Giuseppe Farina won the race becoming the oldest driver to win a Formula 1 race without sharing a car, it was his last Formula 1 win.

Alberto Ascari's Swiss Grand Prix win was his last win and podium finish in a Ferrari. The Italian Grand Prix was the last race under Formula 2 regulations.

1954 Formula 1 Season

Juan Manuel Fangio won the championship.

The Dutch Grand Prix was originally scheduled to be held at Zandvoort, but the circuit had financial troubles so the race was cancelled. Alberto Ascari had left Ferrari for the newly formed Lancia team. It took the whole season for the Lancia cars to be ready, the team managed to have them ready for the final race of the season. Juan Manuel Fnagio moved to Mercedes, a new works team. The team was returning to Grand Prix racing, they had a streamlined single seater with closed wheel body.

Juan Manuel Fangio won the Argentine Grand Prix, his first home win. Pole position was won by 47 year old Giuseppe Farina who became the oldest driver to score a pole position. He finished second in the race, his 17th podium finish.

Stirling Moss first podium finish came at the Belgian Grand prix. During the race Roberto Mieres car caught fire, he managed to get out of the car with only minor burns. Emmanuel de Graffenried car was used to film authentic race footage for the film The Racers.

Mercedes made their debut at the French Grand Prix, Germn driver Karl Kling made his Formula 1 debut with Mercedes. Juan Manuel Fangio had moved to Mercedes and he won the race. Karl King finished on the podium on his debut and was the first German driver to finish on the podium. Mercedes were the first German team to win a Formula 1 race and the first non Italian constructor to win a race.

The Vanwall team made their formula 1 debut at the British Grand Prix, Ron Flockhart and Horace Gould made their debuts as well.

The German Grand Prix lasted 3 hours 45 minutes, it was the longest Formula 1 race until the 2011 Canadian Grand Prix. Argentinian driver Onofre Marimon died during practice at the German Grand Prix. He failed to negotiate a corner and plunged down an embankment, the car somersaulted and Marimon was killed instantly. It was the first driver fatality at a Formula 1 weekend, team mate Luigi Villoresi withdrew from the race.

The Swiss Grand Prix was the last motor race held in Switzerland. After the 1955 Le Mans disaster the Swiss government banned all forms of motor racing in the country. The ban was lifted in 2015, but only for electric vehicles. The Spanish Grand Prix was the last Formula 1 race held at the Pedralbes Street Circuit. The Lancia cars were finally ready in time for the Spanish Grand Prix.

1955 Formula 1 Season

Juan Manuel Fangio won the championship.

Mercedes dominated the season but they left the championship at the end of the season. They didn't return to Formula 1 until 1994 when they entered as an engine supplier and in 2010 they returned as a constructor. Mercedes left Formula 1 after the manufacturer was involved in a fatal disaster at the 24 Hours of Le Mans race. After the Le Mans diaster the French, German, Spanish and Swiss Grand Prix were cancelled. Motor racing was banned in Switzerland and the Pedralbes and Bremgarten circuits were abandoned and never used again.

The Argentine Grand Prix was the hottest Formula 1 race ever held with temperatures of 40 degrees centigrade. The very hot and dry weather was very taxing for the drivers and cars. Only two drivers completed the full race without handing the car to another driver, Argentinians Juan Manuel Fangio and Roberto Mieres. During the race there were 16 driver substitutions. Juan Manuel Fangio suffered severe burns to his legs which was rubbing against the chassis frame which was being heated by the exhaust.

Maurice Trintigant won the Monaco Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win and he was the first French driver to win a Formula 1 race. Alberto Ascari crashed through the barriers into the Monaco harbour and had to swim back to safety. Louis was the oldest driver to start a Grand Prix at 55. The race was Ascari's last Formula 1 race, he died four days later during a test at Monza for Ferrari. He died on the 26th May 1955, his father had died during the French Grand Prix on 26th July 1925.

The Lancia team with drrew from Formula 1 after Alberto Ascari's death.

Stirling Moss won the British Grand Prix, he was the first British driver to win the British Grand Prix. Jack Brabham made his Formula 1 debut at the race.

Juan Manuel Fangio won the Italian Grand Prix in a Mercedes, it was the last race for Mercedes as a constructor until 2010. It was the last Formula 1 race for the first Formual 1 World Champion Giuseppe Farina.

1956 Formula 1 Season

Juan Manuel Fangio won the championship, it was his third consecutive world championship.

It was the last season until 2006 that no British constructor won a Formula 1 race. Juan Manuel Fangio re-joined Ferrari.

Luigi Musso and Juan Manuel Fangio won the Argentine Grand Prix due to sharing a car, it was the first Formula 1 win for Musso. Argentinian drivers Olivier Gendebein and Oscar Gonzalez made their Formula 1 debuts at the race.

Spanish driver Alfonso de Portago scored his first Formula 1 points and podium finish at the British Grand Prix, he was the first Spanish driver to finish on the podium. Alfonso de Portago was the 11th Marquees of Portago in Spain.

Jo Bonnier and Wolfgang Von Trips made their Formual 1 debuts at the Italian Grand Prix, Juan Manuel Fangio and Peter Collins were fighting for the championship going into the race.

1957 Formula 1 Season

Juan Manuel Fangio won the Championship.

Juan Manuel Fangio won his fourth consecutive title and his fifth in total, this record was not beaten until 2003 by Micheal Schumacher. Fangio moved to Maserati and Stirling Moss moved to Vanwall, they were the only drivers who won races during the season. Ferrari failed to win a race during the season both Ferrari driver Eugenio Castelloti and Alfonso de Portago were killed in crashes during the season, neither was during Formula 1 races. Castelloti was killed during testing for Ferrari at Modena after the Argentine Grand Prix, Alfonso de Portago was killed during the Milla Miglia race.

The Suez crisis affected the Dutch and Spanish Grand Prix which were cancelled.

Juan Manuel Fangio retired from Formula 1 at the end of the season and the Maserati team pulled out of Formula 1 at the end of the season.

Masten Gregory was the first American to score a Formula 1 podium finish when he finished on the podium at the Monaco Grand Prix and the Vanwall team scored their first Formula 1 podium. It was Masten Gregory's Formula 1 debut, Tony Brooks also scored his first podium finish at the Monaco Grand Prix.

Herbert Mackay-Fraser made his Formula 1 debut at the French Grand Prix, it was his only Formula 1 Grand Prix as he died a week later during a Formula 2 race at Reims.

Stirling Moss and Tony Brooks won the British Grand Prix, it was the first win for Brooks and also the first Formula 1 win for the Vanwall team. The Vanwall was the first British built car to win a Formula 1 Grand Prix.

The organisers of the German Grand Prix opened the field to Formula 2 cars, they were not eligible for championship points, some of the Formula 2 entrants included Jack Brabham, Carel Godin de Beaufort and Roy Salvadori. Juan Manuel Fangio clinched the championship after winning the German Grand Prix with two races to go, it was his 24th and last Formula 1 victory.

The Pescara was the only Formula 1 race held at the circuit and it was the longest circuit to stage a Formula 1 race at 25.8km long. The Pescara Grand Prix was the first of two consecutive races held in Italy, it was the first time two Formula 1 race were held in the same country in the same year. The Grand Prix was held due to the sudden cancellation of the Belgian and Dutch Grand Prix.

The Pescara track was one of the most difficult and dangerous, Enzo Ferrari did not send the Ferrari team to the race, but Luigi Musso persuaded him to enter a single car. Three months earlier 11 people had been killed during the Milli Miglia including Ferrari driver Alfonso de Portago. During the race Jack Brabham stopped at a roadside petrol station to tap up as he was running short of fuel and was far from the pits.

It was the final time the Italian Grand Prix was the season finale.

1958 Formula 1 Season

Mike Hawthorn won the championship and Vanwall won the constructors title.

The International Cup for Formula 1 Manufacturers was introduced, Vanwall won the first Manufacturers Championship.

It was the last season that the field was dominated by front engined cars, the race lengths were reduced to 300km or 2 hours, which ever came first. The use of commercial petrol became compulsory replacing the specialised alcohol-based racing fuels.

Mike Hawthorn won only one race during the season, the only other driver to win the championship having won only one race during the season was Finnish driver Keke Rosberg in 1982. Juan Manuel Fangio raced in two races during the season as a privateer. Maria Teresa de Filippis became the first female driver to compete in Formula 1.

The Dutch, Belgian and Spanish Grand Prix were cancelled again due to the Suez crisis.

There were four fatalities during the season, Luigi Musso died during the French Grand Prix at Reims, Peter Collins died during the German Grand Prix, Stuart Lewis-Evans died during the Moroccan Grand Prix and Pat O'Connor died during the Indianapolis 500. Mike Hawthorn retired at the end of the season and died in a car crash in November.

Peter Collins was the only driver to retire their car during the Argentine Grand Prix. Stirling Moss won the race driving a Rob Walker privately entered Cooper which was a rear-engined car, it was the first Formula 1 win for a rear-engined car. It was also the first Formula 1 win for a privateer team.

At the age of 58 Louis Chiron entered the Monaco Grand Prix, it was his last Grand Prix, he failed to qualify for the race. Bernie Ecclestone entered the race but also failed to qualify, Chiron and Ecclestone were two of the 15 cars that failed to qualify for the race. Graham Hill and Team Lotus made their Formula 1 debuts at the Monaco Grand Prix, Maurice Trintigant won the race, it was his final Formula 1 win and the second win for the Rob Walker Racing Team.

The Belgian Grand Prix had returned to the calendar, the Spa circuit had been upgraded and the pits had been rebuilt. The pit straight had been widened and straightened and the track was resurfaced. Tony Brooks won the race, it was his first Formula one win where he did not share a car. The podium was an all British podium, female driver Maria Teresa de Filippis made her debut, the first female driver to race in a Formula 1 race.

Mike Hawthorn's first Formula 1 win since 1954 Spanish Grand Prix was at the French Grand Prix. Carroll Shelby made his Formula 1 debut at the race and it was Juan Manuel Fangio's final Formula 1 race. Luigi Musso crashed at the Muizon hairpin, he hurtled off the track and crashed into a ditch. Musso was thrown from the car, he died later in hospital. The British Grand Prix podium was all British, Peter Collins won the race, it was his final Grand Prix win.

The German Grand Prix was open to Formula 2 cars, Jack Brabham, Phil Hill, Bruce Mclaren, Carel Godin de Beaufort and Graham Hill all entered in Formula 2 car. Itwas the first Formula 1 race not to feature an Italian driver, on lap 11 Peter Collins ran too wide and crashed into a ditch and went flying into the air. The car somersaulted and Collins struck a tree head first, he died later in hospital. Bruce Mclaren finished the race 5th, the first of the Formula 2 drivers, the fifth place Formula 1 car finished behind five Formula 2 cars. 11 cars were classified, of which six were Formula 2 cars.

Mike Hawthorn was initially disqualified for restarting his car against the direction of circuit, Stirling Moss having seen the incident went to the judges to try to revert the decision, eventually Mike Hawthorn was classified and retained his second place.

Vanwall won the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix.

There was a six week gap between the Italian and Moroccan Grand Prix, it was the only time that a Formula 1 race was held in Morocco. On lap 41 Stuart Lewis-Evans engine broke sending him off the track, the car caught fire. Lewis-Evans managed to escape the wreckage but was badly burnt, he was airlifted back to Britain but died six days later. Tony Vandervell ended his involvement with the Vanwall team, Bernie Ecclestone sold his Connaught team and cars and ceased involvement in Formula 1 until 1965.

1959 Formula 1 Season

Jack Brabham won the championship and Cooper won the constructors title.

Vanwall had withdrawn from Formula 1 and there was no world champion competing in 1959, Jack Brabham became the first Australian driver to win the championship. Cooper fielded a rear-engined cars, Aston Martin entered Formula 1 but the car was outdated and overweight.

The Argentine Grand Prix was cancelled due to no local interest as Juan Manuel Fangio and Jose Froilan Gonzalez had retired, the Belgian Grand Prix was cancelled due to a dispute over money. Th Moroccan Grand Prix was cancelled for monetary reasons.

Jack Brabham won the Monaco Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 win and the first win for the Cooper factory team. Jo Bonnier scored his only Formula 1 win at the Dutch Grand Prix. Bonnier's win was the first Formula 1 win for a Swedish driver and the first Formula 1 win for the Owen Racing Organisation Team. The Dutch Grand Prix organisers wanted a local driver in the race so Carel Godin de Beaufort was allowed to compete despite his car being a Porsche sports car.

At the French Grand Prix the track started to break up due to the hot weather, as the race went on the cars dislodged stones. During the race Jean Behra's engine broke, he had a heated discussion with his team manager Romolo Tavoni, it ended with Behra puncing his manager. It was his last race for Ferrari as he was fired after the race.

The British Grand Prix had the largest entry list of the season with 30 entries, 24 cars qualified for the race.

Dan Gurney first podium position was at the German Grand Prix, Jean Behra died the day before the German Grand Prix in a Formula 2 support race. The race was held on the AVUS circuit in Berlin, which was the Automobil Verkehrs-Und Ubungsstrasse.

The Portuguese Grand Prix moved to Circuito de Monsanto, Cooper secured the constructors title at the Italian Grand Prix.

The united States Grand Prix was the only Formula 1 race held at Sebring International Raceway which was a former military base. It was the last race until the 1994 Monaco Grand Prix where there was no former Formula 1 champion in the race. Bruce Mclaren was the first New Zealander to win a Formula 1 race and he was the youngest Formula 1 race winner at the age of 22. Jack Brabham, Stirling Moss and Tony Brooks went into the race battling for the championship, Tony Brookes finished third but it was not enough to clinch the championship and Jack Brabham clinched the championship.
