2006 Formula 1 Season

Fernando Alonso won the Championship and Renault won the constructors title.

It was the 60th season of Formula 1 racing and the 57th Formula 1 World Championship season. Fernando Alonso was the youngest double world champion.

It was the first time since 1956 that no British constructor won a race, like 1956 only factory teams won races.

Minardi, BAR and Jordan changed ownership, Minardi became Toro Rosso and Jordan became Spyker MFI Racing. Honda owned 45% of the BAR team and tookover the team which became Honda Racing Formula 1 team. BMW had split with Williams and purchased 80% of Sauber which became BMW Sauber. Super Aguri enterd formula one, the team was owned by former Formula 1 driver Suzuki Aguri.

Felipe Massa moved from Sauber to Ferrari to replace Rubens Barrichello who had moved to Honda. Nick Heidfeld replaced Massa at BMW Sauber, Robert Kubica became the third driver at BMW Sauber. The Super Aguri team had an all Japanese lineup with Takuma Sato and Yuji Ide. Heikki Kovalainen became Renault's third driver, Nico Rosberg made his Formula 1 debut with Williams. Gary Paffett became a permanent testing role at Mclaren and Christijan Albers moved to MFI, Vitantonio Liuzzi moved from Red Bull to Toro Rosso where he partnered Scott Speed.

After the San Marino Grand Prix Franck Montagny replaced Yuji Ide at Super Aguri after Ide had his superlicence revoked. After the United States Grand Prix Juan Pablo Montoya announced his moved to NASCAR in 2007, the following day Mclaren announced Montoya would be replaced by test driver Pedro de la Rosa. Robert Kubica replaced Jacques Villeneuve at BMW Sauber at the Hungarian Grand Prix, the day after the Hungarian Grand Prix BMW Sauber announced Villeneuve had left the team with immediate effect. Sebastian Vettel became BMW Sauber third driver. The Belgian Grand Prix was dropped from the calendar.

Nico Rosberg made his Formula 1 debut at the Bahrain Grand Prix, he is the son of Former World Champion Keke Rosberg. Scott Speed and YujiIde made their Formula 1 debuts, Williams, Honda, Red Bull, BMW-Sauber, Super Aguri, MFI and Toro Rosso were entitled to run a third driver in Friday practice. Alexander Wurz was William's third driver, Antony Davidson for Honda, Robert Doornbos for Red Bull

2007 Formula 1 Season

Kimi Raikkonen won the Championship and Ferrari won the constructors title.

Kimi Raikkonen won the Championship by 1 point at the final race of the season. He was the third Finnish driver to win the World Championship. Raikkonen entered the final race third in the championship and won the race and the championship.

Lewis Hamilton recorded nine consecutive podium finishes in his debut season, more than any other rookie in Fomrula 1 history.

Both Lewis Hamilton and Fernado Alonso ended on 109 points, Lewis Hamilton was placed second through countback.

There was espionage controversy involving Ferrari and Mclaren, which led to Mclaren being excluded from the constructors championship. As a result Ferrari won the championship. Honda ran with an Earth Livery, it was the first time since 1968 that a team ran sponsor-free for the entire season. The Renault cars were uncompetitive. It was the last championship won by a Ferrari driver and the last drivers championship won by a Finnish driver.

Fernando Alonso moved to Mclaren from Renault, Alexander Wurz replaced Mark Webber at Williams. Webber had moved to Red Bull to partner David Coulthard. Robert Kubica retained his seat at BMW Sauber and was partnered by rookie Timo GLock, Heikki Kovalainen replaced Fernado Alonso at Renault. Kimi Raikkonen moved to Ferrari to replace the retired Michael Schumacher, Lewis Hamilton made his Formula 1 debut with Mclaren. Antony Davidson replaced Japanese driver Sakon Yamamoto at Super Aguri, rookie Adrian Sutil replaced Tiago Monteiro at Spyker.

Sponsors Mild Seven, Lucky Strike and 555 left Formula 1, Vodafone became the title sponsors of Mclaren. ING replaced Mild Seven as the main sponsor of Renault and AT and T became the main spnsors of Williams. The United Arab Emirates Airline Company Etihad Airways became Spyker's new sponsors. Red Bull became an Austrian constructor.

The cars were fitted with red, blue and yellow cockpit lights to give the drivers information concerning track signals, there was also a warning light connected to the data logger to indicate the accident severity of the car.

Only Honda, BMW-Sauber and Williams had test drivers for Friday practice.

On his Formula 1 debut Lewis Hamilton finished third after qualifying fourth. It was the first race in Formula 1 when all three podium finishers were in their first race with their new team, Kimi Raikkonen won his first race as a Ferrari driver and Fernado Alonso and Lewis Hamilton for Mclaren.

Fernado Alonso won the Malaysian Grand Prix and Lewis Hamilton fininshed second, it was the first Mclaren 1-2 finish since the 2005 Brazilian Grand Prix Lewis Hamilton became the first driver to finish on the podium in all his first three races, after just four Formula 1 races Hamilton was leading the drivers championship.

Six days before the Monaco Grand Prix, Ferrari employee Nigel Stepney tried to sabotage Kimi Raikkonen's and Felipe Massa's car when a powder was found in the fuel tank of the cars. Parts were replaced before the race, the powder was sent to the police. Stepney was angry that he had not been promoted to technical director.

Lewis Hamilton's first Formula 1 win came at the Canadian Grand Prix, it was the first race in 2007 that drivers from teams other than Ferrari and Mclaren finished on the podium. The safety car was deployed four times during the Canadian Grand Prix. Felipe Massa and Giancarlo Fisichella were disqualifired from the race for exiting the pit lane when the red light was on. Fernando Alonso was disappointed with the outcome of the Canadian Grand Prix as he fininshed seveth and did not congratulate his team mate Lewis Hamilton on his madien Formula 1 win.

Sebastian Vettel made his Fromula 1 debut with BMW Sauber at the United States Grand Prix replacing injured Robert Kubica. It was the last United States Grand Prix until 2012. Ferrari launched a criminal investigation in Modena against Nigel Stepney. Robert Kubica returned for the French Grand Prix.

In the run up to the British Grand Prix Ferrari presented a legal case against engineer Nigel Stepney and a Mclaren employee after a search warrant revealed evidence of theft of technical information from the Italian team.

Originally the European Grand Prix was meant to be the German Grand Prix, it changed to the European Grand Prix due to the Hockenheimring holding the naming rights for the Germand Grand Prix. It was the first time since 1960 that a Formula 1 Grand Prix was not held under the name German Grand Prix. Christijan Albers was replaced at Spyker by Markus Winkelhock, the son of former Fomrula 1 driver Manfred Winkelhock. Albers had failed to pay sponsorship money. The race was red flagged due to weather conditions, it was the first race red flagged since the 2003 Brazilian Grand Prix, the race was restarted later.

Sakon Yamamoto replaced Markus Winkelhock from the Hungarian Grand Prix and Sebastian Vettel replaced Scott Speed at Toro Rosso. During the race Lewis Hamilton failed to honour ageement to let team mate Fernando Alonso passed on track, Alonso retaliated by holding Hamilton up in the pit lane to deny Hamilton the chance to record a final lap time. Fernando Alonso was dropped five places on the grid. Mclaren lost all their constructor's points as a penalty for espionage scandal.

The Japanese Grand Prix returned to Fuji Speedway for the first time since 1977. Heikki Kovalainen scored his first podium finish and Adrian Sutil scored Spykers first and only points. There was poor transportation to the circuit and free practice on Saturday was abandoned due to fog, the road on the east gate of the circuit sank around 16:00 on Saturday meaning that the shuttle buses could not leave. On the Sunday it was announced that spectators who bought seats in the reserved seat area on C strand near the first corner would be refunded due to low visibility. The cost of the refund was expected to be 350 million Yen ($3 million), on the 16 June 2008 109 spectators sued the circuit for compensation. Only Toyota fans were allowed to set up flags and banners at the race.

Immediately after the Chinese Grand Prix Alexander Wurz announced his immediate retirement from Formula 1. At the Brazilian Grand Prix Kimi Raikkonen won the championship, beating Lewis Hamilton by a single point. Rubens Barrichello failed to finish his home Grand Prix, it was the first time since 2003 that he failed to finish in his home Grand Prix. Kazuki Nakajima, the son of former Fomula 1 driver Satoru Nakajima replaced Alexander Wurz at Williams. It was the first time a three way fight for the title since 1986 with Lewis Hamilton, Fernando Alonso and Kimi Raikkonen. It was the last Formula 1 race for Ralf Schumacher who left for DTM in Germany.

2008 Formula 1 Season

Lewis Hamilton won the championship and Ferrari won the

Lewis Hamilton won the championship by a point, overtaking Timo GLock on the final corner of the final lap of the final race of the season. Hamilton became the youngest World Champion and the first black driver to win the championship and the first British champion since Damon Hill in 1996

There were two new street circuits, the Singapore Grand Prix held at the Marina Bay Street Circuit, the first Formula 1 race held at night and the European Grand Prix which moved to the Valencia Street Circuit.

Two teams pulled out of the championship due to financial troubles. Super Aguri withdrew in May and Honda pulled out at the end of the season.

Indian businessman Vijay Mallya bought the Spyker team and renamed it Force India. It was the first Indian team in Formula 1, the team signed German Adrian Sutil and Italian Giancarlo Fisichella, and Vitantonio Liuzzi was the test driver. Fernando Alonso returned to Renault and Heikki Kovalainen replaced him at McLaren and rookie Nelson Piquet Jr partnered Alonso at Renault. Timo GLock replaced Ralf Schumacher at Toyota, Schumacher left Fomrula 1 for DTM in Germany. Christian Klein and Estonian Marko Asmer became test drivers at BMW Sauber. Sebastien Bourdais joined Toro Rosso.

At the Australian Grand Prix there were three safety car interruptions. Nick Heidfeld scored his first Formula 1 podium since the 2007 Hungarian Grand Prix. Rubens Barrichello was disqualified due to ignoring the red light at the pit lane exit.

Before the Spanish Grand Prix the FIA put the circuit on probation due to racist taunting during the pre-season testing at the circuit. It was the the final Formula 1 race for Super Aguri, the team withdrew from the cahmpionship after the Spanish Grand Prix.

Lewis Hamilton won the Monaco Grand Prix, it was his first Formula 1 Monaco win. At the Canadian Grand Prix Lewis Hamilton had to retire on lap 19 after crashing into the back of Kimi Raikkonen in the pit lane. The racr was the last Formula 1 win for a BMW powered car. During qualifying at the Canadian Grand Prix the track started to disintegrate and work to the track was carried out overnight but during the race the circuit was severely degraded.

The French Grand Prix was the last for 10 years, Jarno Trulli scored his first Formula 1 podium since the 2005 Spanish Grand Prix and it was Toyota's first podium since the 2005 Australian Grand Prix. Nelson Piquet Jr scored his first Formula 1 points at the French Grand Prix.

Nelson Piquet Jr scored his first Formula 1 podium at the German Grand Prix, finishing second in the race after starting 17th on the grid. 16 drivers fininshed on the lead lap, this beat the previous record of 14 at the 1999 Belgian Grand Prix. Felipe Massa fininshed in third place it was the first time two Brazilians fininshed on the podium since the 1991 Belgian Grand Prix.

Hekki Kovalainen won the Hungarian Grand Prix, becoming the 100th driver to win a Formula 1 Grand Prix. It was his only Formula 1 win, Timo Glock scored his first Formula 1 podium.

The European Grand Prix was held at the Valencia Street Circuit, a new circuit for the Formula 1 calendar. The Italian Grand Prix started behind the safety car and was won by Toro Rosso driver Sebastian Vettel, the first win for both team and driver. Vettel became the youngest driver to win a Formula 1 race.

The first Singapore GRand Prix brought the first Formula 1 night race. During the race Nelson Piquet Jr deliberately crashed on lap 14 which brought out the safety car. The Renault team did not admit to the deliberate crash until September 2009. Piquet had deliberately crashed per instructions from the Renault team principal Flavio Briatore and Chief engineer Pat Symonds in hope of helping Fernando Alonso win the race. After the truth behind the crash came out the Renault team was disqualified from Formula 1 which was suspended for two years and Briatore was banned from all FIA sanctioned events for life and Symonds was banned for five years. Briatore and Symonds sued in the French courts and in 2010 the bans were overturned.

Renault's last win came at the Japanese Grand Prix. Felipe Massa won the Brazilian Grand Prix, it was his last Formula 1 win. He thought he had won the championship when he won the race but on the last corner of the last lap Lewis Hamilton managed to pass Timo Glock and fininshed fifth and secure the Formula 1 championship.

2009 Formula 1 season

Jenson Button won the championship and Brawn GP won the Constructors title

It was Jenson Button and Brawn GP first and only World championship, Brawn GP was the first team to win the title in their debut season. Jenson Button was the 10th British Formula 1 World Champion and it was the first time the championship was won by English drivers in consecutive seasons.
Ross Brawn had bought the Honda team and renamed it Brawn GP and retained the drivers Jenson Button and Rubens Barrichello.

The Japanese Grand Prix changed from Fuji to Suzuka and the Canadian and French Grand Prix were dropped from the calendar and a new race was added, the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix at the Yas Marina Circuit.

Sebastian Vettel replaced David Coulthard at Red Bull, he was replaced at Torro Rosso by Sebastien Buemi. After the German Grand Prix Sebastien Bourdais was dropped by Torro Rosso, he was replaced by teenager Jaime Alguersuari who had only just replaced Brendon Hartley as the Torro Rosso test driver two weeks prior.

Brawn GP was the first constructor since Mercedes Benz at the 1954 French Grand Prix to qualify on pole then go on to win the race in their debut race. It was the second Formula 1 race to finish behind the safety car and it was the first race since 1997 European Grand Prix to use slick tyres.
The Malaysian Grand Prix did not reach the 75% distance of 42 laps for the full points to be awarded so half points were awarded.

Sebastian Vettel won the Chinese Grand Prix, the first win for Red Bull. Mark Webber won the German Grand Prix, his first Formula 1 win.
Spanish driver Jaime Alguersuari made his Formula 1 debut at the Hungarian Grand Prix, he became the youngest driver at 19 years 125 days. He replaced Sebastien Bourdais at Torro Rosso.
Lewis Hamilton won the Hungarian Grand Prix, his first win since the 2008 Chinese Grand Prix. During qualifying Felipe Massa was struck on his helmet by debris from another car, the force knocked him unconcious which ended Massa's season.

Rubens Barrichello won the European Grand Prix, it was his first win since the 2004 Chinese Grand Prix. It was the 100th win for a Brazilian driver. Romain Grosjean made his Formula 1 debut replacing the sacked Nelson Piquet at Renault and Luca Badoer returned to Formula 1 after ten years away to replace the injured Felipe Massa, his last race was the 1999 Japanese Grand Prix.

Giancarlo Fisichella won pole position and finished second at the Belgian Grand Prix, it was the first and only pole for Force India and their first championship points and podium. Kimi Raikkonens won the race, the first Formula 1 win since the 2008 Spanish Grand Prix.
Brawn GP last Formula 1 win came at the Italian Grand Prix which was won by Rubens Barrichello, it was his final win in Formula 1 and the last for a Brazilian driver.

At the Japanese Grand Prix eight of the drivers had not raced at Suzuka before. Kamui Kobayashi replaced Timo Glock for Friday practice due to Glock having a fever. Glock returned in time for qualifying, but he crashed heavily during qualifying and did not take part in the race.

Jenson Button won his only Formula 1 World Championship and Brawn sealed the constructors title at the Brazilian Grand Prix.

The first Abu-Dhabi Grand Prix took place at the Yas Marina Circuit, it was the first day-night race in Formula 1. It was the last Formula 1 race for Giancarlo Fisichella, Brawn GP, BMW and Toyota.
